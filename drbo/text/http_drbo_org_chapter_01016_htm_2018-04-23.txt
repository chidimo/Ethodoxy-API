             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Abram marrieth Agar, who bringeth forth Ismael.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Now Sarai the wife of Abram, had brought forth no children; but having a handmaid, an Egyptian, named Agar,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               She said to her husband: Behold, the Lord hath restrained me from bearing: go in unto my handmaid, it may be I may have children of her at least. And when he agreed to her request,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               She took Agar the Egyptian her handmaid, ten years after they first dwelt in the land of Chanaan, and gave her to her husband
               <u>
                to wife
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And he went in to her. But she, perceiving that she was with child, despised her mistress.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And Sarai said to Abram: Thou dost unjustly with me: I gave my handmaid into thy bosom, and she perceiving herself to be with child, despiseth me. The Lord judge between me and thee.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [3]
               <u>
                "To wife"
               </u>
               : Plurality of wives, though contrary to the primitive institution of marriage, Gen. 2. 24, was by divine dispensation allowed to the patriarchs: which allowance seems to have continued during the time of the law of Moses. But Christ our Lord reduced marriage to its primitive institution. Matt. 19.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               And Abram made answer, and said to her: Behold thy handmaid is in thy own hand, use her as it pleaseth thee. And when Sarai afflicted her, she ran away.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               And the angel of the Lord having found her, by a fountain of water in the wilderness, which is in the way to Sur in the desert,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               He said to her: Agar, handmaid of Sarai, whence comest thou? and whither goest thou? And she answered: I flee from the face of Sarai, my mistress.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               And the angel of the Lord said to her: Return to thy mistress, and humble thyself under her hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               And again he said: I will multiply thy seed exceedingly, and it shall not be numbered for multitude.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And again: Behold, said he, thou art with child, and thou shalt bring forth a son: and thou shalt call his name Ismael, because the Lord hath heard thy affliction.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               He shall be a wild man: his hand will be against all men, and all men's hands against him: and he shall pitch his tents over against all his brethren.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And she called the name of the Lord that spoke unto her: Thou the God who hast seen me. For she said: Verily here have I seen the hinder parts of him that seeth me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Therefore she called that well, The well of him that liveth and seeth me. The same is between Cades and Barad.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               And Agar brought forth a son to Abram: who called his name Ismael.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=1&amp;ch=16&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Abram was four score and six years old when Agar brought him forth Ismael.
              </p>

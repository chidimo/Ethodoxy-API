     <td class="textarea" valign="top">
      <p class="desc">
       The Israelites are multiplied in Egypt. They are oppressed by a new king, who commandeth all their male children to be killed.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=1-#x">
        [1]
       </a>
       These are the names of the children of Israel, that went into Egypt with Jacob: they went in, every man with his household:
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=2-#x">
        [2]
       </a>
       Ruben, Simeon, Levi, Juda,
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=3-#x">
        [3]
       </a>
       Issachar, Zabulon, and Benjamin,
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=4-#x">
        [4]
       </a>
       Dan, and Nephtali, Gad and Aser.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=5-#x">
        [5]
       </a>
       And all the souls that came out of Jacob's thigh, were seventy: but Joseph was in Egypt.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=6-#x">
        [6]
       </a>
       After he was dead, and all his brethren, and all that generation,
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=7-#x">
        [7]
       </a>
       The children of Israel increased, and sprung up into multitudes, and growing exceedingly strong they filled the land.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=8-#x">
        [8]
       </a>
       In the mean time there arose a new king over Egypt, that knew not Joseph:
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=9-#x">
        [9]
       </a>
       And he said to his people: Behold the people of the children of Israel are numerous and stronger than we.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=10-#x">
        [10]
       </a>
       Come, let us wisely oppress them, lest they multiply: and if any war shall rise against us, join with our enemies, and having overcome us, depart out of the land.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=11-#x">
        [11]
       </a>
       Therefore he set over them masters of the works, to afflict them with burdens, and they built for Pharao cities
       <u>
        of tabernacles
       </u>
       , Phithom and Ramesses.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=12-#x">
        [12]
       </a>
       But the more they oppressed them, the more they were multiplied, and increased:
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=13-#x">
        [13]
       </a>
       And the Egyptians hated the children of Israel, and afflicted them and mocked them:
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=14-#x">
        [14]
       </a>
       And they made their life bitter with hard works in clay, and brick, and with all manner of service, wherewith they were overcharged in the works of the earth.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=15-#x">
        [15]
       </a>
       And the king of Egypt spoke to the midwives of the Hebrews: of whom one was called Sephora, the other Phua,
      </p>
      <p class="note">
       [11]
       <u>
        "Of tabernacles"
       </u>
       : Or, of storehouses.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=16-#x">
        [16]
       </a>
       Commanding them: When you shall do the office of midwives to the Hebrew women, and the time of delivery is come: if it be a man child, kill it: if a woman, keep it alive.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=17-#x">
        [17]
       </a>
       But the midwives feared God, and did not do as the king of Egypt had commanded, but saved the men children.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=18-#x">
        [18]
       </a>
       And the king called for them and said: What is that you meant to do, that you would save the men children?
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=19-#x">
        [19]
       </a>
       They answered: The Hebrew women are not as the Egyptian women: for they themselves are skillful in the office of a midwife; and they are delivered before we come to them.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=20-#x">
        [20]
       </a>
       Therefore God dealt well with the midwives: and the people multiplied and grew exceedingly strong.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=21-#x">
        [21]
       </a>
       And
       <u>
        because the midwives feared God
       </u>
       , he built them houses.
       <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=2&amp;ch=1&amp;l=22-#x">
        [22]
       </a>
       Pharao therefore charged all his people, saying: Whatsoever shall be born of the male sex, ye shall cast into the river: whatsoever of the female, ye shall save alive.
       <p class="note">
        [21]
        <u>
         "Because the midwives feared God"
        </u>
        : The midwives were rewarded, not for their lie, which was a venial sin; but for their fear of God, and their humanity: but this reward was only temporal, in building them houses, that is, in establishing and enriching their families.
       </p>
      </p>
     </td>
    </tr>

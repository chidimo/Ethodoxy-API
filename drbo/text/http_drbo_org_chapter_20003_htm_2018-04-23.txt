             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job expresses his sense of the miseries of man's life, by cursing the day of his birth.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               After this Job opened his mouth, and
               <u>
                cursed his day
               </u>
               ,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And he said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Let the day perish wherein I was born, and the night in which it was said: A man child is conceived.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Let that day be turned into darkness, let not God regard it from above, and let not the light shine upon it.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Let darkness, and the shadow of death cover it, let a mist overspread it, and let it be wrapped up in bitterness.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "Cursed his day"
               </u>
               : Job cursed the day of his birth, not by way of wishing evil to any thing of God's creation; but only to express in a stronger manner his sense of human miseries in general, and of his own calamities in particular.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Let a darksome whirlwind seize upon that night, let it not be counted in the days of the year, nor numbered in the months.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Let that night be solitary, and not worthy of praise.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Let them curse it who curse the day. who are ready to raise up a leviathan:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Let the stars be darkened with the mist thereof: let it expect light and not see it, nor the rising of the dawning of the day:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Because it shut not up the doors of the womb that bore me, nor took away evils from my eyes.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Why did I not die in the womb, why did I not perish when I came out of the belly?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Why received upon the knees? why suckled at the breasts?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               For now I should have been asleep and still, and should have rest in my sleep.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               With kings and consuls of the earth, who build themselves solitudes:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Or with princes, that possess gold, and All their houses with silver:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Or as a hidden untimely birth I should not be, or as they that being conceived have not seen the light.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               There the wicked cease from tumult, and there the wearied in strength are at rest.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               And they sometime bound together without disquiet, have not heard the voice of the oppressor.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               The small and great are there, and the servant is free from his master.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Why is light given to him that is in misery, and life to them that are in bitterness of soul?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               That look for death, and it cometh not, as they that dig for a treasure:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               And they rejoice exceedingly when they have found the grave.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               To a man whose way is hidden, and God hath surrounded him with darkness?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Before I eat I sigh: and as overflowing waters, so is my roaring:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               For the fear which I feared hath come upon me: and that which I was afraid of, hath befallen me.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=3&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               Have I not dissembled? have I not kept silence? have I not been quiet? and indignation is come upon me.
              </p>

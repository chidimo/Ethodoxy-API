             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job declares the miseries of man's life: and addresses himself to God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               The life of man upon earth is a warfare, and his days are like the days of a hireling.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               As a servant longeth for the shade, as the hireling looketh for the end of his work;
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               So I also have had empty months, and have numbered to myself wearisome nights.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               If I lie down to sleep, I shall say: When shall arise? and again I shall look for the evening, and shall be filled with sorrows even till darkness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               My flesh is clothed with rottenness and the filth of dust, my skin is withered and drawn together.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               My days have passed more swiftly than the web is cut by the weaver, and are consumed without any hope.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Remember that my life is but wind, and my eyes shall not return to see good things.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Nor shall the sight of man behold me: thy eyes are upon me, and I shall be no more.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               As a cloud is consumed, and passeth away: so he that shall go down to hell shall not come up.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Nor shall he return my more into his house, neither shall his place know him any more.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Wherefore I will not spare my month, I will speak in the affliction of my spirit: I will talk with the bitterness of my soul.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Am I a sea, or a whale, that thou hast enclosed me in a prison?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               If I say: My bed shall comfort me, and I shall be relieved speaking with myself on my couch:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Thou wilt frighten me with dreams and terrify me with visions.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               So that my soul rather chooseth hanging, and my bones death.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               I have done with hope, I shall now live no longer: spare me, for my days are nothing.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               What is a man that thou shouldst magnify him? or why dost thou set thy heart upon him?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Thou visitest him early in the morning, and thou provest him suddenly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               How long wilt thou not spare me, nor suffer me to swallow down my spittle?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               I have sinned: what shall I do to thee, O keeper of men? why hast thou set me opposite to thee, and I am become burdensome to myself?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=7&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Why dost thou not remove my sin, and why dost thou not take away my iniquity? Behold now I shall sleep in the dust: and if thou seek me in the morning, I shall not be.
              </p>
             </td>

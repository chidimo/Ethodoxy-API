             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job declares the shortness of man's days: and professes his belief of a resurrection.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Man born of a woman, living for a short time, is filled with many miseries.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Who cometh forth like a flower, and is destroyed, and fleeth as a shadow, and never continueth in the same state.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And dost thou think it meet to open thy eyes upon such an one, and to bring him into judgment with thee?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Who can make him clean that is conceived of unclean seed? is it not thou who only art?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               The days of man are short, and the number of his months is with thee: thou hast appointed his bounds which cannot be passed.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Depart a little from him, that he may rest, until his wished for day come, as that of the hireling.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               A tree hath hope: if it be cut, it groweth green again, and the boughs thereof sprout.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               If its root be old in the earth, and its stock be dead in the dust:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               At the scent of water, it shall spring, and bring forth leaves, as when it was first planted.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               But man when he shall be dead, and stripped and consumed, I pray you where is he?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               As if the waters should depart out of the sea, and an emptied river should be dried up:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               So man when he is fallen asleep shall not rise again; till the heavens be broken, he shall not awake, nor rise up out of his sleep.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Who will grant me this,
               <u>
                that thou mayst protect me in hell
               </u>
               , and hide me till thy wrath pass, and appoint me a time when thou wilt remember me?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Shall man that is dead, thinkest thou, live again? all the days in which I am now in warfare, I expect until my change come.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Thou shalt call me, and I will answer thee: to the work of thy hands thou shalt reach out thy right hand.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [13]
               <u>
                "That thou mayst protect me in hell"
               </u>
               : That is, in the state of the dead; and in the place where the souls are kept waiting for their Redeemer.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Thou indeed hast numbered my steps, but spare my sins.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Thou hast sealed up my offences as it were in a bag, but hast cured my iniquity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               A mountain falling cometh to nought, and a rock is removed out of its place.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Waters wear away the stones, and with inundation the ground by little and little is washed away: so in like manner thou shalt destroy man.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Thou hast strengthened him for a little while, that he may pass away for ever: thou shalt change his face, and shalt send him away.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Whether his children come to honour or dishonour, he shall not understand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=14&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               But yet his flesh, while he shall live, shall have pain, and his soul shall mourn over him.
              </p>

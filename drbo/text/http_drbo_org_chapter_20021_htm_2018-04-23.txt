             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job shews that the wicked often prosper in this world, even to the end of their life: but that their judgment is in another world.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Then Job answered, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Hear, I beseech you, my words, and do penance.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Suffer me, and I will speak, and after, if you please, laugh at my words.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Is my debate against man, that I should not have just reason to be troubled?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Hearken to me and be astonished, and lay your finger on your mouth.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               As for me, when I remember, I am afraid, and trembling taketh hold on my flesh.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Why then do the wicked live, are they advanced, and strengthened with riches?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Their seed continueth before them, a multitude of kinsmen, and of children's children in their sight.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Their houses are secure and peaceable, and the rod of God is not upon them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Their cattle have conceived, and failed not: their cow has calved, and is not deprived of her fruit.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Their little ones go out like a flock, and their children dance and play.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               They take the timbrel, and the harp, and rejoice at the sound of the organ.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               They spend their days in wealth, and in a moment they go down to hell.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Who have said to God: Depart from us, we desire not the knowledge of thy ways.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Who is the Almighty, that we should serve him? and what doth it profit us if we pray to him?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Yet because their good things are not in their hand, may the counsel of the wicked be far from me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               How often shall the lamp of the wicked be put out, and a deluge come upon them, and he shall distribute the sorrows of his wrath?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               They shall be as chaff before the face of the wind, and as ashes which the whirlwind scattereth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               God shall lay up the sorrow of the father for his children: and when he shall repay, then shall he know.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               His eyes shall see his own destruction, and he shall drink of the wrath of the Almighty.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               For what is it to him what befalleth his house after him: and if the number of his months be diminished by one half?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Shall any one teach God knowledge, who judgeth those that are high?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               One man dieth strong, and hale, rich and happy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               His bowels are full of fat, and his bones are moistened with marrow.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               But another dieth in bitterness of soul without any riches:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               And yet they shall sleep together in the dust, and worms shall cover them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Surely I know your thoughts, and your unjust judgments against me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               For you say: Where is the house of the prince? and where are the dwelling places of the wicked?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               Ask any one of them that go by the way, and you shall perceive that he knoweth these same things.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               Because the wicked man is reserved to the day of destruction, and he shall be brought to the day of wrath.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               Who shall reprove his way to his face? and who shall repay him what he hath done?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               He shall be brought to the graves, and shall watch in the heap of the dead.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               He hath been
               <u>
                acceptable to the gravel of Cocytus
               </u>
               , and he shall draw every man after him, and there are innumerable before him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=21&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               How then do ye comfort me in vain, whereas your answer is shewn to be repugnant to truth?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [33]
               <u>
                "Acceptable to the gravel of Cocytus"
               </u>
               : The Hebrew word, which St. Jerome has here rendered by the name Cocytus, (which the poets represent as a river in hell,) signifies a valley or a torrent: and in this place, is taken for the low region of death and hell: which willingly, as it were, receives the wicked at their death: who are ushered in by innumerable others that have gone before them; and are followed by multitudes above number.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

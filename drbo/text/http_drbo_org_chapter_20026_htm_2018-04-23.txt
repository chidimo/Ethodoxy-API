             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job declares his sentiments of the wisdom and power of God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Then Job answered, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Whose helper art thou? is it of him that is weak? and dost thou hold up the arm of him that has no strength?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               To whom hast thou given counsel? perhaps to him that hath no wisdom, and thou hast shewn thy very great prudence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Whom hast thou desired to teach? was it not him that made life?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Behold the giants groan under the waters, and they that dwell with them.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Hell is naked before him, and there is no covering for destruction.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               He stretched out the north over the empty space, and hangeth the earth upon nothing.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               He bindeth up the waters in his clouds, so that they break not out and fall down together.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He withholdeth the face of his throne, and spreadeth his cloud over it.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               He hath set bounds about the waters, till light and darkness come to an end.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The pillars of heaven tremble, and dread at his beck.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               By his power the seas are suddenly gathered together, and his wisdom has struck the proud one.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               His spirit hath adorned the heavens, and
               <u>
                his obstetric hand brought forth the winding serpent
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=26&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Lo, these things are said in part of his ways: and seeing we have heard scarce a little drop of his word, who shall be able to behold the thunder of his greatness?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [13]
               <u>
                "His obstetric hand brought forth the winding serpent"
               </u>
               : That is, the omnipotent power of God: which brought forth all things created in time, but conceived in the Divine mind from all eternity. The winding serpent, a constellation of fixed stars winding round the north pole, called Draco. This appears from the foregoing part of the same verse, His spirit hath adorned the heavens.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job shews the wonderful change of his temporal estate, from welfare to great calamity.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               <u>
                But now the younger in time
               </u>
               scorn me, whose fathers I would not have set with the dogs of my flock:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               The strength of whose hands was to me as nothing, and they were thought unworthy of life itself.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Barren with want and hunger, who gnawed in the wilderness, disfigured with calamity and misery.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And they ate grass, and barks of trees, and the root of junipers was their food.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Who snatched up these things out of the valleys, and when they had found any of them, they ran to them with a cry.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "But now the younger in time"
               </u>
               : That is, younger than I am, and as it were obscure, when I was conspicuous and in magnificence; they now look down on me.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               They dwelt in the desert places of torrents, and in caves of earth, or upon the gravel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               They pleased themselves among these kind of things, and counted it delightful to be under the briers.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The children of foolish and base men, and not appearing at all upon the earth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Now I am turned into their song, and am become their byword.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               They abhor me, and flee far from me, and are not afraid to spit in my face.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               For he hath opened his quiver, and hath afflicted me, and hath put a bridle into my mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               At the right hand of my rising, my calamities forthwith arose: they have overthrown my feet, and have overwhelmed me with their paths as with waves.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               They have destroyed my ways, they have lain in wait against me, and they have prevailed, and there was none to help.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               They have rushed in upon me, as when a wall is broken, and a gate opened, and have rolled themselves down to my miseries.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               I am brought to nothing: as a wind thou hast taken away my desire: and my prosperity hath passed away like a cloud.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               And now my soul fadeth within myself, and the days of affliction possess me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               In the night my bone is pierced with sorrows: and they that feed upon me, do not sleep.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               With the multitude of them my garment is consumed, and they have girded me about, as with the collar of my coat.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               I am compared to dirt, and am likened to embers and ashes.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               I cry to thee, and thou hearest me not: I stand up, and thou dost not regard me.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Thou art changed to be cruel toward me, and in the hardness of thy hand thou art against me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Thou hast lifted me up, and set me as it were upon the wind, and thou hast mightily dashed me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               I know that thou wilt deliver me to death, where a house is appointed for every one that liveth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               But yet thou stretchest not forth thy hand to their consumption: and if they shall fall down thou wilt save.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               I wept heretofore for him that was afflicted, and my soul had compassion on the poor.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               I expected good things, and evils are come upon me: I waited for light, and darkness broke out.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               My inner parts have boiled without any rest, the days of affliction have prevented me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               I went mourning without indignation; I rose up, and cried in the crowd.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               I was the
               <u>
                brother of dragons
               </u>
               , and companion of ostriches.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               My skin is become black upon me, and my bones are dried up with heat.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [29]
               <u>
                "Brother of dragons"
               </u>
               : Imitating these creatures in their lamentable noise.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=30&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               My harp is turned to mourning, and my organ into the voice of those that weep.
              </p>

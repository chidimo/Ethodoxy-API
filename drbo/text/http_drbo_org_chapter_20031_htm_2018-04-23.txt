             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job, to defend himself from the unjust judgments of his friends, gives a sincere account of his own virtues.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               I made a covenant with my eyes, that I would not so much as think upon a virgin.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               For what part should God from above have in me, and what inheritance the Almighty from on high?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Is not destruction to the wicked, and aversion to them that work iniquity?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Doth not he consider my ways, and number all my steps?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               If I have walked in vanity, and my foot hath made haste to deceit:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Let him weigh me in a just balance, and let God know my simplicity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               If my step hath turned out of the way, and if my heart hath followed my eyes, and if a spot hath cleaved to my hands:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Then let me sow and let another eat: and let my offspring be rooted out.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               If my heart hath been deceived upon a woman, and if I have laid wait at my friend's door:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Let my wife be the harlot of another, and let other men lie with her.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               For this is a heinous crime, and a most grievous iniquity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               It is a fire that devoureth even to destruction, and rooteth up all things that spring.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               If I have despised to abide judgment with my manservant, or my maidservant, when they had any controversy against me:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               For what shall I do when God shall rise to judge? and when he shall examine, what shall I answer him?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Did not he that made me in the womb make him also: and did not one and the same form me in the womb?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               If I have denied to the poor what they desired, and have made the eyes of the widow wait:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               If I have eaten my morsel alone, and the fatherless hath not eaten thereof:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               (For from my infancy mercy grew up with me: and it came out with me from my mother's womb:)
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               If I have despised him that was perishing for want of clothing, and the poor man that had no covering:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               If his sides have not blessed me, and if he were not warmed with the fleece of my sheep:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               If I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, even when I saw myself superior in the gate:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Let my shoulder fall from its joint, and let my arm with its bones be broken.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               For I have always feared God as waves swelling over me, and his weight I was not able to bear.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               If I have thought gold my strength, and have said to fine gold: My confidence:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               If I have rejoiced over my great riches, and because my hand had gotten much.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               <u>
                If I beheld the sun
               </u>
               when it shined, and the moon going in brightness:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               And my heart in secret hath rejoiced, and I have kissed my hand with my mouth:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Which is a very great iniquity, and a denial against the most high God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               If I have been glad at the downfall of him that hated me, and have rejoiced that evil had found him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               For I have not given my mouth to sin, by wishing a curse to his soul.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [26]
               <u>
                "If I beheld the sun"
               </u>
               : If I behold the sun and moon with admiration, knowing them to be created and governed by the power of God, I call on my adversaries to produce any thing against me, whereby I could be charged with worshipping the sun or moon.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               If the men of my tabernacle have not said: Who will give us of his flesh that we may be filled?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               The stranger did not stay without, my door was open to the traveller.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               If as a man I have hid my sin, and have concealed my iniquity in my bosom.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               If I have been afraid at a very great multitude, and the contempt of kinsmen hath terrified me: and I have not rather held my peace, and not gone out of the door.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               Who would grant me a hearer, that the Almighty may hear my desire; and that he himself that judgeth would write a book,
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=36-#x">
                [36]
               </a>
               That I may carry it on my shoulder, and put it about me as a crown?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=37-#x">
                [37]
               </a>
               At every step of mine I would pronounce it, and offer it as to a prince.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=38-#x">
                [38]
               </a>
               If my land cry against me, and with it the furrows thereof mourn:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=39-#x">
                [39]
               </a>
               If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, and have afflicted the soul of the tillers thereof:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=31&amp;l=40-#x">
                [40]
               </a>
               Let thistles grow up to me instead of wheat, and thorns instead of barley.
              </p>
              <p>

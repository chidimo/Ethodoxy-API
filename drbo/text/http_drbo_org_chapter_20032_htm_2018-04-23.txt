             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Eliu is angry with Job and his friends. He boasts of himself.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               So these three men ceased to answer Job, because he seemed just to himself.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And Eliu the son of Barachel the Buzite, of the kindred of Ram, was angry and was moved to indignation: now he was angry against Job, because he said he was just before God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And he was angry with his friends because they had not found a reasonable answer, but only had condemned Job.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               So Eliu waited while Job was speaking, because they were his elders that were speaking.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               But when he saw that the three were not able to answer, he was exceedingly angry.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Then Eliu the son of Barachel the Buzite answered and said: I am younger in days, and you are more ancient; therefore hanging down my head, I was afraid to shew you my opinion.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               For I hoped that greater age would speak, and that a multitude of years would teach wisdom.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               But, as I see, there is a spirit in men, and the inspiration of the Almighty giveth understanding.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               They that are aged are not the wise men, neither do the ancients understand judgment.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Therefore I will speak: Hearken to me, I also will shew you my wisdom.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               For I have waited for your words, I have given ear to your wisdom, as long as you were disputing in words.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               And as long as I thought you said some thing, I considered: but, as I see, there is none of you that can convince Job, and answer his words.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Lest you should say: We have found wisdom, God hath cast him down, not man.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               He hath spoken nothing to me, and I will not answer him according to your words.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               They were afraid, and answered no more, and they left off speaking.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Therefore because I have waited, and they have not spoken: they stood, and answered no more:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               I also will answer my part, and will shew my knowledge.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               For I am full of matter to speak of, and the spirit of my bowels straiteneth me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Behold, my belly is as new wine which wanteth vent, which bursteth the new vessels.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               I will speak and take breath a little: I will open my lips, and will answer.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               I will not accept the person of man, and
               <u>
                I will not level God with man
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=32&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               For I know not how long I shall continue, and whether after a while my Maker may take me away.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [21]
               <u>
                "I will not level God with man"
               </u>
               : Here Eliu considers that Job hath put himself on a level with God, by the manner he assumed to justify his own life in speaking to God as if he spoke to an equal: Eliu expresses in the following ver. 22 his fear of punishment hereafter for such an attempt.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

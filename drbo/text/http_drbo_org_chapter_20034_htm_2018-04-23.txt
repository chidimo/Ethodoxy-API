             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Eliu charges Job with blasphemy: and sets forth the power and justice of God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               And Eliu continued his discourse, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Hear ye, wise men, my words, and ye learned, hearken to me:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               For the ear trieth words, and the mouth discerneth meats by the taste.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Let us choose to us judgment, and let us see among ourselves what is the best.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               For Job hath said: I am just, and God hath overthrown my judgment.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               For in judging me there is a lie: my arrow is violent without any sin.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               What man is there like Job, who drinketh up scorning like water?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Who goeth in company with them that work iniquity, and walketh with wicked men?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               For he hath said: Man shall not please God, although he run with him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Therefore, ye men of understanding, hear me: far from god be wickedness, and iniquity from the Almighty.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               For he will render to a man his work, and according to the ways of every one he will reward them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               For in very deed God will not condemn without cause, neither will the Almighty pervert judgment.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               What other hath he appointed over the earth? or whom hath he set over the world which he made?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               If he turn his heart to him, he shall draw his spirit and breath unto himself.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               All flesh shall perish together, and man shall return into ashes.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               If then thou hast understanding, hear what is said, and hearken to the voice of my words.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Can he be healed that loveth not judgment? and how dost thou so far condemn him that is just?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Who saith to the king: Thou art an apostate: who calleth rulers ungodly?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Who accepteth not the persons of princes: nor hath regarded the tyrant, when he contended against the poor man: for all are the work of his hands.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               They shall suddenly die, and the people shall be troubled at midnight, and they shall pass, and take away the violent without hand.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               For his eyes are upon the ways of men, and he considereth all their steps.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               There is no darkness, and there is no shadow of death, where they may be hid who work iniquity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               For it is no longer in the power of man to enter into judgment with God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               He shall break in pieces many and innumerable, and shall make others to stand in their stead.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               For he knoweth their works: and therefore he shall bring night on them, and they shall be destroyed.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               He hath struck them, as being wicked, in open sight.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Who as it were on purpose have revolted from him, and would not understand all his ways:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               So that they caused the cry of the needy to come to him, and he heard the voice of the poor.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               For when he granteth peace, who is there that can condemn? When he hideth his countenance, who is there that can behold him, whether it regard nations, or all men?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               Who maketh a man that is a hypocrite to reign for the sins of the people?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               Seeing then I have spoken of God, I will not hinder thee in thy turn.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               If I have erred, teach thou me: if I have spoken iniquity, I will add no more.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               Doth God require it of thee, because it hath displeased thee? for thou begannest to speak, and not I: but if thou know any thing better, speak.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               Let men of understanding speak to me, and let a wise man hearken to me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               But Job hath spoken foolishly, and his words sound not discipline.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=36-#x">
                [36]
               </a>
               My father, let Job be tried even to the end: cease not from the man of iniquity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=34&amp;l=37-#x">
                [37]
               </a>
               Because he addeth blasphemy upon his sins, let him be tied fast in the mean time amongst us: and then let him provoke God to judgment with his speeches.
              </p>
             </td>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Of the power of God in the behemoth and the leviathan.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               And the Lord answering Job out of the whirlwind, said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Gird up thy loins like a man: I will ask thee, and do thou tell me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Wilt thou make void my judgment: and condemn me, that thou mayst be justified?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And hast thou an arm like God, and canst thou thunder with a voice like him?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Clothe thyself with beauty, and set thyself up on high and be glorious, and put on goodly garments.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Scatter the proud in thy indignation, and behold every arrogant man, and humble him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Look on all that are proud, and confound them, and crush the wicked in their place.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Hide them in the dust together, and plunge their faces into the pit.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Then I will confess that thy right hand is able to save thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Behold
               <u>
                behemoth
               </u>
               whom I made with thee, he eateth grass like an ox.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [10]
               <u>
                "Behemoth"
               </u>
               : In Hebrew, behema, which signifies in general an animal; but many authors explain, that here it is put for the elephant.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               His strength is in his loins, and his force in the navel of his belly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               He setteth up his tail like a cedar, the sinews of his testicles are wrapped together.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               His bones are like pipes of brass, his gristle like plates of iron.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               He is the beginning of the ways of God, who made him,
               <u>
                he will apply his sword
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               To him the mountains bring forth grass: there all the beasts of the field shall play.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [14]
               <u>
                "He will apply his sword"
               </u>
               : This text is variously explained: some explain the sword, the horn given to the animal for his defence: others, the power that God hath given to the animal for his defence: others, the power that God hath given to man to slay him, notwithstanding his great size and strength.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               He sleepeth under the shadow, in the covert of the reed, and in moist places.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               The shades cover his shadow, the willows of the brook shall compass him about.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Behold, he will drink up a river, and not wonder: and he trusteth that the Jordan may run into his mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               In his eyes as with a hook he shall take him, and bore through his nostrils with stakes.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Canst thou draw out the
               <u>
                leviathan
               </u>
               with a hook, or canst thou tie his tongue with a cord?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [20]
               <u>
                "Leviathan"
               </u>
               : The whale or some sea monster.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Canst thou put a ring in his nose, or bore through his jaw with a buckle?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Will he make many supplications to thee, or speak soft words to thee?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Will he make a covenant with thee, and wilt thou take him to be a servant for ever?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Shalt thou play with him as with a bird, or tie him up for thy handmaids?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Shall friends cut him in pieces, shall merchants divide him?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               Wilt thou fill nets with his skin, and the cabins of fishes with his head?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Lay thy hand upon him: remember the battle, and speak no more.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=40&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Behold his hope shall fail him, and in the sight of all he shall be cast down.
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               A further description of the leviathan.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               I will not stir him up, like one that is cruel: for who can resist my countenance?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Who hath given me before that I should repay him? All things that are under heaven are mine.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               I will not spare him, nor his mighty words, and framed to make supplication.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Who can discover the face of his garment? or who can go into the midst of his mouth?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Who can open the doors of his face? his teeth are terrible round about.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               His body is like molten shields, shut close up with scales pressing upon one another.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               One is joined to another, and not so much as any air can come between them:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               They stick one to another and they hold one another fast, and shall not be separated.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               His sneezing is like the shining of fire, and his eyes like the eyelids of the morning.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Out of his mouth go forth lamps, like torches of lighted fire.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Out of his nostrils goeth smoke, like that of a pot heated and boiling.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               His breath kindleth coals, and a flame cometh forth out of his mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               In his neck strength shall dwell, and want goeth before his face.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               The members of his flesh cleave one to another: he shall send lightnings against him, and they shall not be carried to another place.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               His heart shall be as hard as a stone, and as firm as a smith's anvil.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               When he shall raise him up, the
               <u>
                angels
               </u>
               shall fear, and being affrighted shall purify themselves.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               When a sword shall lay at him, it shall not be able to hold, nor a spear, nor a breastplate.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               For he shall esteem iron as straw, and brass as rotten wood.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               The archer shall not put him to flight, the stones of the sling are to him like stubble.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               As stubble will he esteem the hammer, and he will laugh him to scorn who shaketh the spear.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [16]
               <u>
                "Angels"
               </u>
               : Elim, Hebrew: which signifies here, the mighty, the most valiant, shall fear this monstrous fish, and in their fear shall seek to be purified.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               The beams of the sun shall be
               <u>
                under him
               </u>
               , and he shall strew gold under him like mire.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               He shall make the deep sea to boil like a pot, and shall make it as when ointments boil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               A path shall shine after him, he shall esteem
               <u>
                the deep as growing old
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               There is no power upon earth that can be compared with him who was made to fear no one.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=41&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               He beholdeth every high thing,
               <u>
                he is king
               </u>
               over all the children of pride.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [21]
               <u>
                "Under him"
               </u>
               : He shall not value the beams of the sun; and gold to him shall be like mire.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [23]
               <u>
                "The deep as growing old"
               </u>
               : Growing hoary, as it were with the froth which he leaves behind him.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [25]
               <u>
                "He is king"
               </u>
               : He is superior in strength to all that are great and strong amongst living creatures: mystically it is understood of the devil, who is king over all the proud.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Domine, Deus salutis. A prayer of one under grievous affliction: it agrees to Christ in his passion, and alludes to his death and burial.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               A canticle of a psalm for the sons of Core: unto the end, for
               <u>
                Maheleth
               </u>
               , to answer
               <u>
                understanding
               </u>
               of Eman the Ezrahite.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               O Lord, the God of my salvation: I have cried in the day, and in the night before thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Let my prayer come in before thee: incline thy ear to my petition.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               For my soul is filled with evils: and my life hath drawn nigh to hell.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               I am counted among them that go down to the pit: I am become as a man without help,
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "Maheleth"
               </u>
               : A musical instrument, or chorus of musicians, to answer one another.-- Ibid.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "Understanding"
               </u>
               : Or a psalm of instruction, composed by Eman the Ezrahite, or by David, in his name.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Free among the dead. Like the slain sleeping in the sepulchres, whom thou rememberest no more: and they are cast off from thy hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               They have laid me in the lower pit: in the dark places, and in the shadow of death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Thy wrath is strong over me: and all thy waves thou hast brought in upon me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Thou hast put away my acquaintance far from me: they have set me an abomination to themselves. I was delivered up, and came not forth:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               My eyes languished through poverty. All the day I cried to thee, O Lord: I stretched out my hands to thee.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Wilt thou shew wonders to the dead? or shall physicians raise to life, and give praise to thee?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Shall any one in the sepulchre declare thy mercy: and thy truth in destruction?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Shall thy wonders be known in the dark; and thy justice in the land of forgetfulness?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               But I, O Lord, have cried to thee: and in the morning my prayer shall prevent thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Lord, why castest thou off my prayer: why turnest thou away thy face from me?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               I am poor, and in labours from my youth: and being exalted have been humbled and troubled.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Thy wrath hath come upon me: and thy terrors have troubled me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               They have come round about me like water all the day: they have compassed me about together.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=21&amp;ch=87&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Friend and neighbour thou hast put far from me: and my acquaintance, because of misery.
              </p>

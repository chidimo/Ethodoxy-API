             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               An exhortation to the practice of virtue.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               My son, forget not my law, and let thy heart keep my commandments.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               For they shall add to thee length of days, and years of life and peace.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Let not mercy and truth leave thee, put them about thy neck, and write them in the tables of thy heart:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And thou shalt find grace and good understanding before God and men.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Have confidence in the Lord with all thy heart, and lean not upon thy own prudence.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               In all thy ways think on him, and he will direct thy steps.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Be not wise in thy own conceit: fear God, and depart from evil:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               For it shall be health to thy navel, and moistening to thy bones.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Honour the Lord with thy substance, and give him of the first of all thy fruits:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               And thy barns shall be filled with abundance, and thy presses shall run over with wine.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               My son, reject not the correction of the Lord: and do not faint when thou art chastised by him:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               For whom the Lord loveth, he chastiseth: and as a father in the son he pleaseth himself.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Blessed is the man that findeth wisdom and is rich in prudence:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               The purchasing thereof is better than the merchandise of silver, and her fruit than the chiefest and purest gold:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               She is more precious than all riches: and all the things that are desired, are not to be compared with her.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Length of days is in her right hand, and in her left hand riches and glory.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Her ways are beautiful ways, and all her paths are peaceable.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               She is a tree of life to them that lay hold on her: and he that shall retain her is blessed.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               The Lord by wisdom hath founded the earth, hath established the heavens by prudence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               By his wisdom the depths have broken out, and the clouds grow thick with dew.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               My son, let not these things depart from thy eyes: keep the law and counsel:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               And there shall be life to thy soul, and grace to thy mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Then shalt thou walk confidently in thy way, and thy foot shall not stumble:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               If thou sleep, thou shalt not fear: thou shalt rest, and thy sleep shall be sweet.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Be not afraid of sudden fear, nor of the power of the wicked falling upon thee.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               For the Lord will be at thy side, and will keep thy foot that thou be not taken.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Do not withhold him from doing good, who is able: if thou art able, do good thyself also.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Say not to thy friend: Go, and come again: and tomorrow I will give to thee: when thou canst give at present.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               Practise not evil against thy friend, when he hath confidence in thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               Strive not against a man without cause, when he hath done thee no evil.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               Envy not the unjust man, and do not follow his ways:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               For every mocker is an abomination to the Lord, and his communication is with the simple.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               Want is from the Lord in the house of the wicked: but the habitations of the just shall be blessed.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               He shall scorn the scorners, and to the meek he will give grace.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=3&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               The wise shall possess glory: the promotion of fools is disgrace.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               An exhortation to fly unlawful lust, and the occasions of it.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               My son, attend to my wisdom, and incline thy ear to my prudence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               That thou mayst keep thoughts, and thy lips may preserve instruction. Mind not the deceit of a woman.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               For the lips of a harlot are like a honeycomb dropping, and her throat is smoother than oil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               But her end is bitter as wormwood, and sharp as a two-edged sword.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Her feet go down into death, and her steps go in as far as hell.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               They walk not by the path of life, her steps are wandering, and unaccountable.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Now therefore, my son, hear me, and depart not from the words of my mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Remove thy way far from her, and come not nigh the doors of her house.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Give not thy honour to strangers, and thy years to the cruel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Lest strangers be filled with thy strength, and thy labours be in another man's house,
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And thou mourn it the last, when thou shalt have spent thy flesh and thy body, and say:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Why have I hated instruction, and my heart consented not to reproof,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And have not heard the voice of them that taught me, and have not inclined my ear to masters?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               I have almost been in all evil, in the midst of the church and of the congregation.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Drink water out of thy own cistern, and the streams of thy own well:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Let thy fountains be conveyed abroad, and in the streets divide thy waters.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Keep them to thyself alone, neither let strangers be partakers with thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Let thy vein be blessed, and rejoice with the wife of thy youth:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Let her be thy dearest hind, and most agreeable fawn: let her breasts inebriate thee at all times; be thou delighted continually with her love.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Why art thou seduced, my son, by a strange woman, and art cherished in the bosom of another?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               The Lord beholdeth the ways of man, and considereth all his steps.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               His own iniquities catch the wicked, and he is fast bound with the ropes of his own sins.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=5&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               He shall die, because he hath not received instruction, and in the multitude of his folly he shall be deceived.
              </p>

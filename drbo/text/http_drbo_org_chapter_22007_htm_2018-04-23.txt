             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               The love of wisdom is the best preservative from being led astray by temptation.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               My son, keep my words, and lay up my precepts with thee. Son,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Keep my commandments, and thou shalt live: and my law as the apple of thy eye:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Bind it upon thy fingers, write it upon the tables of thy heart.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Say to wisdom: Thou art my sister: and call prudence thy friend,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               That she may keep thee from the woman that is not thine, and from the stranger who sweeteneth her words.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               For I look out of the window of my house through the lattice,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               And I see little ones, I behold a foolish young man,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Who passeth through the street by the corner, and goeth nigh the way of her house.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               In the dark, when it grows late, in the darkness and obscurity of the night,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               And behold a woman meeteth him in harlot's attire prepared to deceive souls; talkative and wandering,
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Not bearing to be quiet, not able to abide still at home,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Now abroad, now in the streets, now lying in wait near the corners.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And catching the young man, she kisseth him, and with an impudent face, flattereth, saying:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               I vowed victims for prosperity, this day I have paid my vows.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Therefore I am come out to meet thee, desirous to see thee, and I have found thee.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               I have woven my bed with cords, I have covered it with painted tapestry, brought from Egypt.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               I have perfumed my bed with myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Come, let us be inebriated with the breasts, and let us enjoy the desired embraces, till the day appear.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               For my husband is not at home, he is gone a very long journey.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               He took with him a bag of money: he will return home the day of the full moon.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               She entangled him with many words, and drew him away with the flattery of her lips.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Immediately he followeth her as an ox led to be a victim, and as a lamb playing the wanton, and not knowing that he is drawn like a fool to bonds,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Till the arrow pierce his liver: as if a bird should make haste to the snare, and knoweth not that his life is in danger.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Now therefore, my son, hear me, and attend to the words of my mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Let not thy mind be drawn away in her ways: neither be thou deceived with her paths.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               For she hath cast down many wounded, and the strongest have been slain by her.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=7&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Her house is the way to hell, reaching even to the inner chambers of death.
              </p>

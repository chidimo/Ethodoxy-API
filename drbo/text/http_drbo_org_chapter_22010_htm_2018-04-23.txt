             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               In the twenty following chapters are contained many wise sayings and axioms, relating to wisdom and folly, virtue and vice.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               A wise son maketh the father glad: but a foolish son is the sorrow of his mother.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Treasures of wickedness shall profit nothing: but justice shall deliver from death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               The Lord will not afflict the soul of the just with famine, and he will disappoint the deceitful practices of the wicked.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               The slothful hand hath wrought poverty: but the hand of the industrious getteth riches. He that trusteth to lies feedeth the winds: and the same runneth after birds that fly away.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               He that gathered in the harvest is a wise son: but he that snorteth in the summer, is the son of confusion.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               The blessing of the Lord is upon the head of the just: but iniquity covereth the mouth of the wicked.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               The memory of the just is with praises: and the name of the wicked shall rot.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The wise of heart receiveth precepts: a fool is beaten with lips.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He that walketh sincerely, walketh confidently: but he that perverteth his ways, shall be manifest.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               He that winketh with the eye shall cause sorrow: and the foolish in lips shall be beaten.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The mouth of the just is a vein of life: and the mouth of the wicked covereth iniquity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Hatred stirreth up strifes: and charity covereth all sins.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               In the lips of the wise is wisdom found: and a rod on the back of him that wanteth sense.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Wise men lay up knowledge: but the mouth of the fool is next to confusion.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               The substance of a rich man is the city of his strength: the fear of the poor is their poverty.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               The work of the just is unto life: but the fruit of the wicked, unto sin.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               The way of life, to him that observeth correction: but he that forsaketh reproofs goeth astray.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Lying lips hide hatred: he that uttereth reproach is foolish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               In the multitude of words there shall not want sin: but he that refraineth his lips is most wise.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               The tongue of the just is as choice silver: but the heart of the wicked is nothing worth.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               The lips of the just teach many: but they that are ignorant, shall die in the want of understanding.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               The blessing of the Lord maketh men rich: neither shall affliction be joined to them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               A fool worketh mischief as it were for sport: but wisdom is prudence to a man.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               That which the wicked feareth, shall come upon him: to the just their desire shall be given.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               As a tempest that passeth, so the wicked shall be no more: but the just is as an everlasting foundation.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               As vinegar to the teeth, and smoke to the eyes, so is the sluggard to them that sent him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               The fear of the Lord shall prolong days: and the years of the wicked shall be shortened.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               The expectation of the just is joy; but the hope of the wicked shall perish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               The strength of the upright is the way of the Lord: and fear to them that work evil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               The just shall never be moved: but the wicked shall not dwell on the earth.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               The mouth of the just shall bring forth wisdom: the tongue of the perverse shall perish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=10&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               The lips of the just consider what is acceptable: and the mouth of the wicked uttereth perverse things.
              </p>
             </td>

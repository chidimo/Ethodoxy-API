             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               A deceitful balance is an abomination before the Lord: and a just weight is his will.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Where pride is, there also shall be reproach: but where humility is, there also is wisdom.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               The simplicity of the just shall guide them: and the deceitfulness of the wicked shall destroy them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Riches shall not profit in the day of revenge: but justice shall deliver from death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               The justice of the upright shall make his way prosperous: and the wicked man shall fall by his own wickedness.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               The justice of the righteous shall deliver them: and the unjust shall be caught in their own snares.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               When the wicked man is dead, there shall be no hope any more: and the expectation of the solicitous shall perish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The just is delivered out of distress: and the wicked shall be given up for him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               The dissembler with his mouth deceiveth his friend: but the just shall be delivered by knowledge.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               When it goeth well with the just the city shall rejoice: and when the wicked perish there shall be praise.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               By the blessing of the just the city shall be exalted: and by the mouth of the wicked it shall be overthrown.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               He that despiseth his friend, is mean of heart: but the wise man will hold his peace.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               He that walketh deceitfully, revealeth secrets: but he that is faithful, concealeth the thing committed to him by his friend.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Where there is no governor, the people shall fall: but there is safety where there is much counsel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               He shall be afflicted with evil, that is surety for a stranger: but he that is aware of the snares, shall be secure.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               A gracious woman shall find glory: and the strong shall have riches.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               A merciful man doth good to his own soul: but he that is cruel casteth off even his own kindred.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               The wicked maketh an unsteady work: but to him that soweth justice, there is a faithful reward.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Clemency prepareth life: and the pursuing of evil things, death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               A perverse heart is abominable to the Lord: and his will is in them that walk sincerely.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Hand in hand the evil man shall not be innocent: but the seed of the just shall be saved.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               A golden ring in a swine's snout, a woman fair and foolish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               The desire of the just is all good: the expectation of the wicked is indignation.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Some distribute their own goods, and grow richer: others take away what is not their own, and are always in want.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               The soul which blesseth, shall be made fat: and he that inebriateth, shall be inebriated also himself.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               He that hideth up corn, shall be cursed among the people: but a blessing upon the head of them that sell.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Well doth he rise early who seeketh good things; but he that seeketh after evil things shall be oppressed by them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               He that trusteth in his riches shall fall: but the just shall spring up as a green leaf.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               He that troubleth his own house, shall inherit the winds: and the fool shall serve the wise.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               The fruit of the just man is a tree of life: and he that gaineth souls, is wise.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=11&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               If the just man receive in the earth, how much more the wicked and the sinner.
              </p>
             </td>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               A mild answer breaketh wrath: but a harsh word stirreth up fury.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               The tongue of the wise adorneth knowledge: but the mouth of fools bubbleth out folly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               The eyes of the Lord in every place behold the good and the evil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               A peaceable tongue is a tree of life: but that which is immoderate, shall crush the spirit.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               A fool laugheth at the instruction of his father: but he that regardeth reproofs shall become prudent. In abundant justice there is the greatest strength: but the devices of the wicked shall be rooted out.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               The house of the just is very much strength: and in the fruits of the wicked is trouble.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               The lips of the wise shall disperse knowledge: the heart of fools shall be unlike.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The victims of the wicked are abominable to the Lord: the vows of the just are acceptable.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               The way of the wicked is an abomination to the Lord: he that followeth justice is beloved by him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Instruction is grievous to him that forsaketh the way of life: he that hateth reproof shall die.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Hell and destruction are before the Lord: how much more the hearts of the children of men?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               A corrupt man loveth not one that reproveth him: nor will he go to the wise.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               A glad heart maketh a cheerful countenance: but by grief of mind the spirit is cast down.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               The heart of the wise seeketh instruction: and the mouth of fools feedeth on foolishness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               All the days of the poor are evil: a secure mind is like a continual feast.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Better is a little with the fear of the Lord, than great treasures without content,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               It is better to be invited to herbs with love, than to a fatted calf with hatred.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               A passionate man stirreth up strifes: he that is patient appeaseth those that are stirred up.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               The way of the slothful is as a hedge of thorns; the way of the just is without offence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               A wise son maketh a father joyful: but the foolish man despiseth his mother.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Folly is joy to the fool: and the wise man maketh straight his steps.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Designs are brought to nothing where there is no counsel: but where there are many counsellors, they are established.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               A man rejoiceth in the sentence of his mouth: and a word in due time is best.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               The path of life is above for the wise, that he may decline from the lowest hell.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               The Lord will destroy the house of the proud: and will strengthen the borders of the widow.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               Evil thoughts are an abomination to the Lord: and pure words most beautiful shall be confirmed by him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               He that is greedy of gain troubleth his own house: but he that hateth bribes shall live. By mercy and faith sins are purged away: and by the fear of the Lord every one declineth from evil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               The mind of the just studieth obedience: the mouth of the wicked over floweth with evils.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               The Lord is far from the wicked: and he will hear the prayers of the just.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               The light of the eyes rejoiceth the soul: a good name maketh the bones fat.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               The ear that heareth the reproofs of life, shall abide in the midst of the wise.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               He that rejecteth instruction, despiseth his own soul: but he that yieldeth to reproof possesseth understanding.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=15&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               The fear of the Lord is the lesson of wisdom: and humility goeth before glory.
              </p>
             </td>

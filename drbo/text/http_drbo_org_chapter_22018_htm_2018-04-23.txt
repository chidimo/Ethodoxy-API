             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               He that hath a mind to depart from a friend seeketh occasions: he shall ever be subject to reproach.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               A fool receiveth not the words of prudence: unless thou say those things which are in his heart.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               The wicked man when he is come into the depth of sins, contemneth: but ignominy and reproach follow him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Words from the mouth of a man are as deep water: and the fountain of wisdom as an overflowing stream.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               It is not good to accept the person of the wicked, to decline from the truth of judgment.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               The lips of a fool intermeddle with strife: and his mouth provoketh quarrels.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               The mouth of a fool is his destruction: and his lips are the ruin of his soul.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The words of the double tongued are as if they were harmless: and they reach even to the inner parts of the bowels. Fear casteth down the slothful: and the souls of the effeminate shall be hungry.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He that is loose and slack in his work, is the brother of him that wasteth his own works.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               The name of the Lord is a strong tower: the just runneth to it, and shall be exalted.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The substance of the rich man is the city of his strength, and as a strong wall compassing him about.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Before destruction, the heart of a man is exalted: and before he be glorified, it is humbled.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               He that answereth before he heareth sheweth himself to be a fool, and worthy of confusion.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               The spirit of a man upholdeth his infirmity: but a spirit that is easily angered, who can bear?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               A wise heart shall acquire knowledge: and the ear of the wise seeketh instruction.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               A man's gift enlargeth his way, and maketh him room before princes.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               The just is first accuser of himself: his friend cometh, and shall search him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               The lot suppresseth contentions, and determineth even between the mighty.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               A brother that is helped by his brother, is like a strong city: and judgments are like the bars of cities.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Of the fruit of a man's mouth shall his belly be satisfied: and the offspring of his lips shall fill him.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Death and life are in the power of the tongue: they that love it, shall eat the fruits thereof.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               He that hath found a good wife, hath found a good thing, and shall receive a pleasure from the Lord. He that driveth away a good wife, driveth away a good thing: but he that keepeth an adulteress, is foolish and wicked.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               The poor will speak with supplications, and the rich will speak roughly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=18&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               A man amiable in society, shall be more friendly than a brother.
              </p>
             </td>

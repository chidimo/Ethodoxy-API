              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               The wicked man fleeth, when no man pursueth: but the just, bold as a lion, shall be without dread.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               For the sins of the land many are the princes thereof: and for the wisdom of a man, and the knowledge of those things that are said, the life of the prince shall be prolonged.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               A poor man that oppresseth the poor, is like a violent shower, which bringeth a famine.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               They that forsake the law, praise the wicked man: they that keep it, are incensed against him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Evil men think not on judgment: but they that seek after the Lord, take notice of all things.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Better is the poor man walking in his simplicity, than the rich in crooked ways.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               He that keepeth the law is a wise son: but he that feedeth gluttons, shameth his father.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               He that heapeth together riches by usury and loan, gathereth them for him that will be bountiful to the poor.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He that turneth away his ears from hearing the law, his prayer shall be as abomination.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               He that deceiveth the just in a wicked way, shall fall in his own destruction: and the upright shall possess his goods.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The rich man seemeth to himself wise: but the poor man that is prudent shall search him out.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               In the joy of the just there is great glory: when the wicked reign, men are ruined.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               He that hideth his sins, shall not prosper: but he that shall confess, and forsake them, shall obtain mercy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Blessed is the man that is always fearful: but he that is hardened in mind, shall fall into evil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               As a roaring lion, and a hungry bear, so is a wicked prince over the poor people.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               A prince void of prudence shall oppress many by calumny: but he that hateth covetousness, shall prolong his days.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               A man that doth violence to the blood of a person, if he flee even to the pit, no man will stay him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               He that walketh uprightly, shall be saved: he that is perverse in his ways shall fall at once.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               He that tilleth his ground, shall be filled with bread: but he that followeth idleness shall be filled with poverty.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               A faithful man shall be much praised: but he that maketh haste to be rich, shall not be innocent.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               He that hath respect to a person in judgment, doth not well: such a man even for a morsel of bread forsaketh the truth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               A man, that maketh haste to be rich, and envieth others, is ignorant that poverty shall come upon him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               He that rebuketh a man, shall afterward find favour with him, more than he that by a flattering tongue deceiveth him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               He that stealeth any thing from his father, or from his mother: and saith, This is no sin, is the partner of a murderer.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               He that boasteth, and puffeth up himself, stirreth up quarrels: but he that trusteth in the Lord, shall be healed.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               He that trusteth in his own heart, is a fool: but he that walketh wisely, he shall be saved.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               He that giveth to the poor, shall not want: he that despiseth his entreaty, shall suffer indigence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=28&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               When the wicked rise up, men shall hide themselves: when they perish, the lust shall be multiplied.
              </p>
             </td>

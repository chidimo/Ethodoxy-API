             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               An exhortation to chastity, temperance, and works of mercy; with the praise of a wise woman.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               The words of king
               <u>
                Lamuel
               </u>
               . The vision wherewith his mother instructed him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               What, O my beloved, what, O the beloved of my womb, what, O the beloved of my vows?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Give not thy substance to women, and thy riches to destroy kings.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Give not to kings, O Lamuel, give not wine to kings: because there is no secret where drunkenness reigneth:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And lest they drink and forget judgments, and pervert the cause of the children of the poor.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "Lamuel"
               </u>
               : This name signifies God with him, and is supposed to have been one of the names of Solomon.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Give strong drink to them that are sad: and wine to them that are grieved in mind:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Let them drink, and forget their want, and remember their sorrow no more.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Open thy mouth for the dumb, and for the causes of all the children that pass.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Open thy mouth, decree that which is just, and do justice to the needy and poor.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Who shall find a valiant woman? far and from the uttermost coasts is the price of her.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The heart of her husband trusteth in her, and he shall have no need of spoils.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               She will render him good, and not evil, all the days of her life.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               She hath sought wool and flax, and hath wrought by the counsel of her hands.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               She is like the merchant's ship, she bringeth her bread from afar.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               And she hath risen in the night, and given a prey to her household, and victuals to her maidens.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               She hath considered a field, and bought it: with the fruit of her hands she hath planted a vineyard.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               She hath girded her loins with strength, and hath strengthened her arm.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               She hath tasted and seen that her traffic is good: her lamp shall not be put out in the night.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               She hath put out her hand to strong things, and her fingers have taken hold of the spindle.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               She hath opened her hand to the needy, and stretched out her hands to the poor.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               She shall not fear for her house in the cold of snow: for all her domestics are clothed with double garments.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               She hath made for herself clothing of tapestry: fine linen, and purple is her covering.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Her husband is honourable in the gates, when he sitteth among the senators of the land.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               She made fine linen, and sold it, and delivered a girdle to
               <u>
                the Chanaanite
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Strength and beauty are her clothing, and she shall laugh in the latter day.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [24]
               <u>
                "The Chanaanite"
               </u>
               : The merchant, for Chanaanite, in Hebrew, signifies a merchant.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               She hath opened her mouth to wisdom, and the law of clemency is on her tongue.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               She hath looked well to the paths of her house, and hath not eaten her bread idle.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Her children rose up, and called her blessed: her husband, and he praised her.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               Many daughters have gathered together riches: thou hast surpassed them all.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               Favour is deceitful, and beauty is vain: the woman that feareth the Lord, she shall be praised.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=31&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               Give her of the fruit of her hands: and let her works praise her in the gates.
              </p>

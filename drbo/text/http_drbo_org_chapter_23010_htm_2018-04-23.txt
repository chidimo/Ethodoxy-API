             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Observations on wisdom and folly, ambition and detraction.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Dying flies spoil the sweetness of the ointment. Wisdom and glory is more precious than a small and shortlived folly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               The heart of a wise man is in his right hand, and the heart of a fool is in his left hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Yea, and the fool when he walketh in the way, whereas he himself is a fool, esteemeth all men fools.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               If the spirit of him that hath power, ascend upon thee, leave not thy place: because care will make the greatest sins to cease.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               There is an evil that I have seen under the sun, as it were by an error proceeding from the face of the prince:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               A fool set in high dignity, and the rich sitting beneath.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               I have seen servants upon horses: and princes walking on the ground as servants.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               He that diggeth a pit, shall fall into it: and he that breaketh a hedge, a serpent shall bite him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He that removeth stones, shall be hurt by them: and he that cutteth trees, shall be wounded by them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               If the iron be blunt, and be not as before, but be made blunt, with much labour it shall be sharpened: and after industry shall follow wisdom.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               If a serpent bite in silence, he is nothing better that backbiteth secretly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               The words of the mouth of a wise man are grace: but the lips of a fool shall throw him down headlong.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               The beginning of his words is folly, and the end of his talk is a mischievous error.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               A fool multiplieth words. A man cannot tell what hath been before him: and what shall be after him, who can tell him?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               The labour of fools shall afflict them that know not how to go to the city.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Woe to thee, O land, when thy king is a child, and when the princes eat in the morning.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Blessed is the land, whose king is noble, and whose princes eat in due season for refreshment, and not for riotousness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               By slothfulness a building shall be brought down, and through the weakness of hands, the house shall drop through.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               For laughter they make bread, and wine that the living may feast: and all things obey money.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=23&amp;ch=10&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Detract not the king, no not in thy thought; and speak not evil of the rich man in thy private chamber: because even the birds of the air will carry thy voice, and he that hath wings will tell what thou hast said.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

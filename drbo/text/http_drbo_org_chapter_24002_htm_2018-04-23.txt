             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Christ caresses his spouse: he invites her to him.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               <u>
                I am the flower of the field
               </u>
               , and the lily of the valleys.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               As the lily among thorns, so is my love among the daughters.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               As the apple tree among the trees of the woods, so is my beloved among the sons. I sat down under his shadow, whom I desired: and his fruit was sweet to my palate.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               He brought me into the cellar of wine, he set in order charity in me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Stay me up with flowers, compass me about with apples: because I languish with love.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "I am the flower of the field"
               </u>
               : Christ professes himself the flower of mankind, yea, the Lord of all creatures: and, ver. 2, declares the excellence of his spouse, the true church above all other societies, which are to be considered as thorns.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               His left hand is under my head, and his right hand shall embrace me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               I adjure you, O ye daughters of Jerusalem, by the roes, and the harts of the, fields, that you stir not up, nor make the beloved to awake, till she please.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               <u>
                The voice of my beloved
               </u>
               , behold he cometh leaping upon the mountains, skipping over the hills.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               My beloved is like a roe, or a young hart. Behold he standeth behind our wall, looking through the windows, looking through the lattices.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Behold my beloved speaketh to me: Arise, make haste, my love, my dove, my beautiful one, and come.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "The voice of my beloved"
               </u>
               : that is, the preaching of the gospel surmounting difficulties figuratively here expressed by mountains and little hills.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               For winter is now past, the rain is over and gone.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               The flowers have appeared in our land, the time of pruning is come: the voice of the turtle is heard in our land:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               The fig tree hath put forth her green figs: the vines in flower yield their sweet smell. Arise, my love, my beautiful one, and come:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               My dove in the clefts of the rock, in the hollow places of the wall, shew me thy face, let thy voice sound in my ears: for thy voice is sweet, and thy face comely.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               <u>
                Catch us the little foxes
               </u>
               that destroy the vines: for our vineyard hath flourished.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [15]
               <u>
                "Catch us the little foxes"
               </u>
               : Christ commands his pastors to catch false teachers, by holding forth their fallacy and erroneous doctrine, which like foxes would bite and destroy the vines.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               My beloved to me, and I to him who feedeth among the lilies,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=2&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Till the day break, and the shadows retire. Return: be like, my beloved, to a roe, or to a young hart upon the mountains of Bether.
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Christ sets forth the graces of his spouse: and declares his love for her.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               <u>
                How beautiful art thou
               </u>
               , my love, how beautiful art thou! thy eyes are doves' eyes, besides what is hid within. Thy hair is as flocks of goats, which Come up from mount Galaad.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Thy teeth as flocks of sheep, that are shorn which come up from the washing, all with twins, and there is none barren among them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Thy lips are as a scarlet lace: and thy speech sweet. Thy cheeks are as a piece of a pomegranate, besides that which lieth hid within.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Thy neck, is as the tower of David, which is built with bulwarks: a thousand bucklers hang upon it, all the armour of valiant men.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               <u>
                Thy two breasts
               </u>
               like two young roes that are twins, which feed among the lilies.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "How beautiful art thou"
               </u>
               : Christ again praises the beauties of his church, which through the whole of this chapter are exemplified by a variety of metaphors, setting forth her purity, her simplicity, and her stability.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [5]
               <u>
                "Thy two breasts"
               </u>
               : Mystically to be understood: the love of God and the love of our neighbour, which are so united as twins which feed among the lilies: that is, the love of God and our neighbour, feeds on the divine mysteries and the holy sacraments, left by Christ to his spouse to feed and nourish her children.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Till the day break, and the shadows retire, I will go to the mountain of myrrh, and to the hill of frankincense.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Thou art all fair, O my love, and there is not a spot in thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Come from Libanus, my spouse, come from Libanus, come: thou shalt be crowned from the top of Amana, from the top of Sanir and Hermon, from the dens of the lions, from the mountains of the leopards.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Thou hast wounded my heart, my sister, my spouse, thou hast wounded my heart with one of thy eyes, and with one hair of thy neck.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               How beautiful are thy breasts, my sister, my spouse! thy breasts are more beautiful than wine, and the sweet smell of thy ointments above all aromatical spices.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Thy lips, my spouse, are as a dropping honeycomb, honey and milk are under thy tongue; and the smell of thy garments, as the smell of frankincense.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               My sister, my spouse, is
               <u>
                a garden enclosed
               </u>
               , a garden enclosed,
               <u>
                a fountain sealed up
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Thy plants are a paradise of pomegranates with the fruits of the orchard. Cypress with spikenard.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Spikenard and saffron, sweet cane and cinnamon, with all the trees of Libanus, myrrh and aloes with all the chief perfumes.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               The fountain of gardens: the well of living waters, which run with a strong stream from Libanus.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [12]
               <u>
                "A garden enclosed"
               </u>
               : Figuratively the church is enclosed, containing only the faithful.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [12]
               <u>
                "A fountain sealed up"
               </u>
               : That none can drink of its waters, that is, the graces and spiritual benefits of the holy sacraments, but those who are within its walls.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=4&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Arise, O north wind, and come, O south wind, blow through my garden, and let the aromatical spices thereof flow.
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               The spouse of Christ is but one: she is fair and terrible.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               <u>
                My beloved is gone down into his garden
               </u>
               , to the bed of aromatical spices, to feed in the gardens, and to gather lilies.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               I to my beloved, and my beloved to me, who feedeth among the lilies.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Thou art beautiful, O my love, sweet and comely as Jerusalem: terrible as an army set in array.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Turn away thy eyes from me, for they have made me flee away. Thy hair is as a flock of goats, that appear from Galaad.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Thy teeth as a flock of sheep, which come up from the washing, all with twins, and there is none barren among them.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "My beloved is gone down into his garden"
               </u>
               : Christ, pleased with the good works of his holy and devout servants labouring in his garden, is always present with them: but the words is gone down, are to be understood, that after trying his Church by permitting persecution, he comes to her assistance and she rejoices at his coming.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Thy cheeks are as the bark of a pomegranate, beside what is hidden within thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               There are threescore queens, and fourscore concubines, and young maidens without number.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               <u>
                One is my dove
               </u>
               , my perfect one is but one, she is the only one of her mother, the chosen of her that bore her. The daughters saw her, and declared her most blessed: the queens and concubines, and they praised her.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               <u>
                Who is she
               </u>
               that cometh forth as the morning rising, fair as the moon, bright as the sun, terrible as an army set in array?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               I went down into the garden of nuts, to see the fruits of the valleys, and to look if the vineyard had flourished, and the pomegranates budded.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "One is my dove"
               </u>
               : That is, my church is one, and she only is perfect and blessed.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [9]
               <u>
                "Who is she"
               </u>
               : Here is a beautiful metaphor describing the church from the beginning. As, the morning rising, signifying the church before the written law; fair as the moon, shewing her under the light of the gospel: and terrible as an army, the power of Christ's church against its enemies.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               I knew not: my soul troubled me for the chariots of Aminadab.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=24&amp;ch=6&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Return, return, O Sulamitess: return, return that we may behold thee.
              </p>

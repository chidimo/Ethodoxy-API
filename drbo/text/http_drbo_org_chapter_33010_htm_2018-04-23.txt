             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               After many benefits, great affliction shall fall upon the ten tribes, for their ingratitude to God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Israel a vine full of branches, the fruit is agreeable to it: according to the multitude of his fruit he hath multiplied altars, according to the plenty of his land he hath abounded with idols.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Their heart is divided: now they shall perish: he shall break down their idols, he shall destroy their altars.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               For now they shall say: We have no king: because we fear not the Lord: and what shall a king do to us?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               You speak words of an unprofitable vision, and you shall make a covenant: and judgment shall spring up as bitterness in the furrows of the field.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               The inhabitants of Samaria have worshipped
               <u>
                the kine of Bethaven
               </u>
               : for the people thereof have mourned over it, and the wardens of its temple that rejoiced over it in its glory because it is departed from it.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [5]
               <u>
                "The kine of Bethaven"
               </u>
               : The golden calves of Jeroboam.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               For
               <u>
                itself also is carried
               </u>
               into Assyria, a present to the avenging king: shame shall fall upon Ephraim, and Israel shall be confounded in his own will.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Samaria hath made her king to pass as froth upon the face of the water.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               And the high places of the idol, the sin of Israel shall be destroyed: the bur and the thistle shall grow up over their altars: and they shall say to the mountains: Cover us; and to the hills: Fall upon us.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               From the days of Gabaa, Israel hath sinned, there they stood: the battle in Gabaa against the children of iniquity shall not overtake them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               According to my desire I will chastise them: and the nations shall be gathered together against them, when they shall be chastised for
               <u>
                their two iniquities
               </u>
               .
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [6]
               <u>
                "Itself also is carried"
               </u>
               : One of the golden calves was given by king Manahem, to Phul, king of the Assyrians, to engage him to stand by him.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [10]
               <u>
                "Their two iniquities"
               </u>
               : Their two calves.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Ephraim is a heifer taught to love to tread out corn, but I passed over upon the beauty of her neck: I will ride upon Ephraim, Juda shall plough, Jacob shall break the furrows for himself.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Sow for yourselves in justice, and reap in the mouth of mercy, break up your fallow ground: but the time to seek the Lord is, when he shall come that shall teach you justice.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               You have ploughed wickedness, you have reaped iniquity, you have eaten the fruit of lying: because thou hast trusted in thy ways, in the multitude of thy strong ones.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               A tumult shall arise among thy people: and all thy fortresses shall be destroyed
               <u>
                as Salmana was destroyed, by the house
               </u>
               of him that judged Baal in the day of battle, the mother being dashed in pieces upon her children.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=10&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               So hath Bethel done to you, because of the evil of your iniquities.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [14]
               <u>
                "As Salmana was destroyed, by the house"
               </u>
               : Salmana, king of the Midianites, was destroyed by the house, that is, by the followers of him that judged Baal; that is, of Gideon, who threw down the altar of Baal; and was therefore called Jerubaal. See Judges 6 and 8.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

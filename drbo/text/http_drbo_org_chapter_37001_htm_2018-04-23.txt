           <td class="textarea" valign="top">
            <p class="desc">
             Jonas being sent to preach in Ninive, fleeth away by sea: a tempest riseth: of which he being found, by lot, to be the cause, is cast into the sea, which thereupon is calmed.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=1-#x">
              [1]
             </a>
             Now the word of the Lord came to Jonas the son of Amathi, saying:
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=2-#x">
              [2]
             </a>
             Arise, and go to
             <u>
              Ninive
             </u>
             the great city, and preach in it: for the wickedness thereof is come up before me.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=3-#x">
              [3]
             </a>
             And Jonas rose up to flee into
             <u>
              Tharsis
             </u>
             from the face of the Lord, and he went down to Joppe, and found a ship going to Tharsis: and he paid the fare thereof, and went down into it, to go with them to Tharsis from the face of the Lord.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=4-#x">
              [4]
             </a>
             But the Lord sent a great wind into the sea: and a great tempest was raised in the sea, and the ship was in danger to be broken.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=5-#x">
              [5]
             </a>
             And the mariners were afraid, and the men cried to their god: and they cast forth the wares that were in the ship, into the sea, to lighten it of them: and Jonas went down into the inner part of the ship, and fell into
             <u>
              a deep sleep
             </u>
             .
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [2]
             <u>
              "Ninive"
             </u>
             : The capital city of the Assyrian empire.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [3]
             <u>
              "Tharsis"
             </u>
             : Which some take to be Tharsus of Cilicia, others to be Tartessus of Spain, others to be Carthage.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [5]
             <u>
              "A deep sleep"
             </u>
             : This is a lively image of the insensibility of sinners, fleeing from God, and threatened on every side with his judgments: and yet sleeping as if they were secure.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=6-#x">
              [6]
             </a>
             And the shipmaster came to him, and said to him: Why art thou fast asleep? rise up, call upon thy God, if so be that God will think of us, that we may not perish.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=7-#x">
              [7]
             </a>
             And they said every one to his fellow: Come, and let us cast lots, that we may know why this evil is upon us. And they cast lots, and the lot fell upon Jonas.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=8-#x">
              [8]
             </a>
             And they said to him: Tell us for what cause this evil is upon us, what is thy business? of what country art thou? and whither goest thou? or of what people art thou?
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=9-#x">
              [9]
             </a>
             And he said to them: I am a Hebrew, and I fear the Lord the God of heaven, who made both the sea and the dry land.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=10-#x">
              [10]
             </a>
             And the men were greatly afraid, and they said to him: Why hast thou done this? (for the men knew that he fled from the face of the Lord: because he had told them.)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=11-#x">
              [11]
             </a>
             And they said to him: What shall we do to thee, that the sea may be calm to us? for the sea flowed and swelled.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=12-#x">
              [12]
             </a>
             And he said to them: Take me up, and cast me into the sea, and the sea shall be calm to you: for I know that for my sake this great tempest is upon you.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=13-#x">
              [13]
             </a>
             And the men rowed hard to return to land, but they were not able: because the sea tossed and swelled upon them.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=14-#x">
              [14]
             </a>
             And they cried to the Lord, and said: We beseech thee, O Lord, let us not perish for this man's life, and lay not upon us innocent blood: for thou, O Lord, hast done as it pleased thee.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=15-#x">
              [15]
             </a>
             And they took Jonas, and cast him into the sea, and the sea ceased from raging.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=1&amp;l=16-#x">
              [16]
             </a>
             And the men feared the Lord exceedingly, and sacrificed victims to the Lord, and made vows.
            </p>

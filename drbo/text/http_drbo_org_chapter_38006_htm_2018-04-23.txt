             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               God expostulates with the Jews for their ingratitude and sins: for which they shall be punished.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Hear ye what the Lord saith: Arise, contend thou in judgment against
               <u>
                the mountains
               </u>
               , and let the hills hear thy voice.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Let the mountains hear the judgment of the Lord, and the strong foundations of the earth: for the Lord will enter into judgment with his people, and he will plead against Israel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               O my people, what have I done to thee, or in what have I molested thee? answer thou me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               For I brought thee up out of the land of Egypt, and delivered thee out of the house of slaves: and I sent before thy face Moses, and Aaron, and Mary.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               O my people, remember, I pray thee, what Balach the king of Moab purposed: and what Balaam the son of Beor answered him,
               <u>
                from Setim to Galgal
               </u>
               , that thou mightest know the justices of the Lord.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "The mountains"
               </u>
               : That is, the great ones, the princes of the people.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [5]
               <u>
                "From Setim to Galgal"
               </u>
               : He puts them in mind of the favour he did them, in not suffering them to be quite destroyed by the evil purpose of Balach, and the wicked counsel of Balaam: and then gives them a hint of the wonders he wrought, in order to bring them into the land of Promise, by stopping the course of the Jordan, in their march from Setim to Galgal.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               <u>
                What shall I offer
               </u>
               to the Lord that is worthy? wherewith shall I kneel before the high God? shall I offer holocausts unto him, and calves of a year old?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               May the Lord be appeased with thousands of rams, or with many thousands of fat he goats? shall I give my firstborn for my wickedness, the fruit of my body for the sin of my soul?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               I will shew thee, O man, what is good, and what the Lord requireth of thee: Verily, to do judgment, and to love mercy, and to walk solicitous with thy God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               The voice of the Lord crieth to the city, and salvation shall be to them that fear thy name: hear, O ye tribes, and who shall approve it?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               As yet there is a fire in the house of the wicked, the treasures of iniquity, and a scant measure
               <u>
                full of wrath
               </u>
               .
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [6]
               <u>
                "What shall I offer"
               </u>
               : This is spoken in the person of the people, desiring to be informed what they are to do to please God.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [10]
               <u>
                "Full of wrath"
               </u>
               : That is, highly provoking in the sight of God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Shall I justify wicked balances, and the deceitful weights of the bag?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               By which her rich men were filled with iniquity, and the inhabitants thereof have spoken lies, and their tongue was deceitful in their mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And I therefore began to strike thee with desolation for thy sins.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Thou shalt eat, but shalt not be filled: and thy humiliation shall be in the midst of thee: and thou shalt take hold, but shalt not save: and those whom thou shalt save, I will give up to the sword.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Thou shalt sow, but shalt not reap: thou shalt tread the olives, but shalt not be anointed with the oil: and the new wine, but shalt not drink the wine.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=6&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               For thou hast kept
               <u>
                the statutes of Amri
               </u>
               , and all the works of the house of Achab: and thou hast walked according to their wills, that I should make thee a desolation, and the inhabitants thereof a hissing, and you shall bear the reproach of my people.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [16]
               <u>
                "The statutes of Amri"
               </u>
               : The wicked ways of Amri and Achab, idolatrous kings.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Charity is to be preferred before all gifts.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               If I speak with the tongues of men, and of angels, and have not charity, I am become as sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And if I should have prophecy and should know all mysteries, and all knowledge, and if I should have all faith, so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity, I am nothing.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And if I should distribute all my goods to feed the poor, and if I should deliver my body to be burned, and have not charity, it profiteth me nothing.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Charity is patient, is kind: charity envieth not, dealeth not perversely; is not puffed up;
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Is not ambitious, seeketh not her own, is not provoked to anger, thinketh no evil;
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Rejoiceth not in iniquity, but rejoiceth with the truth;
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Charity never falleth away: whether prophecies shall be made void, or tongues shall cease, or knowledge shall be destroyed.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               For we know in part, and we prophesy in part.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               But when that which is perfect is come, that which is in part shall be done away.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               When I was a child, I spoke as a child, I understood as a child, I thought as a child. But, when I became a man, I put away the things of a child.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               We see now through a glass in a dark manner; but then face to face. Now I know in part; but then I shall know even as I am known.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=53&amp;ch=13&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And now there remain faith, hope, and charity, these three: but the greatest of these is charity.
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               He warns him against heretics, and exhorts him to the exercise of piety.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Now the Spirit manifestly saith, that in the last times some shall depart from the faith, giving heed to spirits of error, and doctrines of devils,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Speaking lies in hypocrisy, and having their conscience seared,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               <u>
                Forbidding to marry, to abstain from meats
               </u>
               , which God hath created to be received with thanksgiving by the faithful, and by them that have known the truth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               For every creature of God is good, and nothing to be rejected that is received with thanksgiving:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               For it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [3]
               <u>
                "Forbidding to marry, to abstain from meats"
               </u>
               : He speaks of the Gnostics, the Marcionites, the Eneratites, the Manicheans, and other ancient heretics, who absolutely condemned marriage, and the use of all kind of meat; because they pretended that all flesh was from an evil principle. Whereas the church of God, so far from condemning marriage, holds it a holy sacrament; and forbids it to none but such as by vow have chosen the better part: and prohibits not the use of any meats whatsoever in proper times and seasons; though she does not judge all kind of diet proper for days of fasting and penance.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               These things proposing to the brethren, thou shalt be a good minister of Christ Jesus, nourished up in the words of faith, and of the good doctrine which thou hast attained unto.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               But avoid foolish and old wives' fables: and exercise thyself unto godliness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               For bodily exercise is profitable to little: but godliness is profitable to all things, having promise of the life that now is, and of that which is to come.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               A faithful saying and worthy of all acceptation.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               For therefore we labor and are reviled, because we hope in the living God, who is the Saviour of all men, especially of the faithful.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               These things command and teach.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Let no man despise thy youth: but be thou an example of the faithful in word, in conversation, in charity, in faith, in chastity.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Till I come, attend unto reading, to exhortation, and to doctrine.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Neglect not the grace that is in thee, which was given thee by prophesy, with imposition of the hands of the priesthood.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Meditate upon these things, be wholly in these things: that thy profiting may be manifest to all.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=61&amp;ch=4&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Take heed to thyself and to doctrine: be earnest in them. For in doing this thou shalt both save thyself and them that hear thee.
              </p>

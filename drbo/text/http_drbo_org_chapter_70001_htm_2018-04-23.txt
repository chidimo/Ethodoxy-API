           <td class="textarea" valign="top">
            <p class="desc">
             He recommends walking in truth, loving one another and to beware of false teachers.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=1-#x">
              [1]
             </a>
             <u>
              The ancient
             </u>
             <u>
              to the lady Elect
             </u>
             , and her children, whom I love in the truth, and not I only, but also all they that have known the truth,
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=2-#x">
              [2]
             </a>
             For the sake of the truth which dwelleth in us, and shall be with us for ever.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=3-#x">
              [3]
             </a>
             Grace be with you, mercy, and peace from God the Father, and from Christ Jesus the Son of the Father; in truth and charity.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=4-#x">
              [4]
             </a>
             I was exceeding glad, that I found of thy children walking in truth, as we have received a commandment from the Father.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=5-#x">
              [5]
             </a>
             And now I beseech thee, lady, not as writing a new commandment to thee, but that which we have had from the beginning, that we love one another.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [1]
             <u>
              "The ancient"
             </u>
             : That is, the ancient bishop St. John, being the only one of the twelve apostles then living.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [1]
             <u>
              "To the lady Elect"
             </u>
             : Some conjecture that Elect might be the name of a family, or of a particular church; but the general opinion is, that it is the proper name of a lady, so eminent for her piety and great charity, as to merit this Epistle from St. John.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=6-#x">
              [6]
             </a>
             And this is charity, that we walk according to his commandments. For this is the commandment, that, as you have heard from the beginning, you should walk in the same:
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=7-#x">
              [7]
             </a>
             For many seducers are gone out into the world, who confess not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh: this is a seducer and an antichrist.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=8-#x">
              [8]
             </a>
             Look to yourselves, that you lose not the things which you have wrought: but that you may receive a full reward.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=9-#x">
              [9]
             </a>
             Whosoever revolteth, and continueth not in the doctrine of Christ, hath not God. He that continueth in the doctrine, the same hath both the Father and the Son.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=10-#x">
              [10]
             </a>
             If any man come to you, and bring not this doctrine, receive him not into the house
             <u>
              nor say to him, God speed you
             </u>
             .
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [10]
             <u>
              "Nor say to him, God speed you"
             </u>
             : This admonition is in general, to forewarn the faithful of the dangers which may arise from a familiarity with those who have prevaricated and gone from the true faith, and with such as teach false doctrine. But this is not forbidding a charity for all men, by which we ought to wish and pray for the eternal salvation of every one, even of our enemies.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=11-#x">
              [11]
             </a>
             For he that saith unto him, God speed you, communicateth with his wicked works.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=12-#x">
              [12]
             </a>
             Having more things to write unto you, I would not by paper and ink: for I hope that I shall be with you, and speak face to face: that your joy may be full.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=70&amp;ch=1&amp;l=13-#x">
              [13]
             </a>
             The children of thy sister Elect salute thee.
            </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job lements his afflictions and begs to be delivered.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               My soul is weary of my life, I will let go my speech against myself, I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               I will say to God: Do not condemn me: tell me why thou judgest me so.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Doth it seem good to thee that thou shouldst calumniate me, and oppress me, the work of thy own hands, and help the counsel of the wicked?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Hast thou eyes of flesh: or, shalt thou see as man seeth?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Are thy days as the days of man, and are thy years as the times of men:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               That thou shouldst inquire after my iniquity, and search after my sin?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               And shouldst know that I have done no wicked thing, whereas there is no man that can deliver out of thy hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Thy hands have made me, and fashioned me wholly round about, and dost thou thus cast me down headlong on a sudden?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the clay, and thou wilt bring me into dust again.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Hast thou not milked me as milk, and curdled me like cheese?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Thou hast clothed me with skin and flesh: thou hast put me together with bones and sinews:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Thou hast granted me life and mercy, and thy visitation hath preserved my spirit.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Although thou conceal these things in thy heart, yet I know that thou rememberest all things.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               If I have sinned and thou hast spared me for an hour: why dost thou not suffer me to be clean from my iniquity?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               And if I be wicked, woe unto me: and if just, I shall not lift up my head, being filled with affliction and misery.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               And for pride thou wilt take me as a lioness, and returning thou tormentest me wonderfully.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Thou renewest thy witnesses against me, and multipliest thy wrath upon me, and pains war against me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Why didst thou bring me forth out of the womb: O that I had been consumed that eye might not see me!
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               I should have been as if I had not been, carried from the womb to the grave.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Shall not the fewness of my days be ended shortly? suffer me, therefore, that I may lament my sorrow a little:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Before I go, and return no more, to a land that is dark and covered with the mist of death:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=10&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               A land of misery and darkness, where the shadow of death, and no order, but everlasting horror dwelleth.
              </p>
             </td>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job persists in maintaining his innocence: and reproves his friends.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Behold my eye hath seen all these things, and my ear hath heard them, and I have understood them all.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               According to your knowledge I also know: neither am I inferior to you.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               But yet I will speak to the Almighty, and I desire to reason with God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Having first shewn that you are forgers of lies, and maintainers of perverse opinions.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And I wish you would hold your peace, that you might be thought to be wise men.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Hear ye therefore my reproof, and attend to the judgment of my lips.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Hath God any need of your lie, that you should speak deceitfully for him?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Do you accept his person, and do you endeavour to judge for God?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Or shall it please him, from whom nothing can be concealed? or shall he be deceived as a man, with your deceitful dealings?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               He shall reprove you, because in secret you accept his person.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               As soon as he shall move himself, he shall trouble you: and his dread shall fall upon you.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Your remembrance shall be compared to ashes, and your necks shall be brought to clay.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Hold your peace a little while, that I may speak whatsoever my mind shall suggest to me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Why do I tear my flesh with my teeth, and carry my soul in my hands?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Although he should kill me, I will trust in him: but yet I will reprove my ways in his sight.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               And he shall be my saviour: for no hypocrite shall come before his presence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Hear ye my speech, and receive with your ears hidden truths.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               If I shall be judged, I know that I shall be found just.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Who is he that will plead against me? let him come: why am I consumed holding my peace?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Two things only do not to me, and then from thy face I shall not be hid:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Withdraw thy hand far from me, and let not thy dread terrify me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Call me, and I will answer thee: or else I will speak, and do thou answer me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               How many are my iniquities and sins? make me know my crimes and offences.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Why hidest thou thy face, and thinkest me thy enemy?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Against a leaf, that is carried away with the wind, thou shewest thy power, and thou pursuest a dry straw.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               For thou writest bitter things against me, and wilt consume me for the sins of my youth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Thou hast put my feet in the stocks, and hast observed all my paths, and hast considered the steps of my feet:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=13&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Who am to be consumed as rottenness, and as a garment that is moth-eaten.
              </p>

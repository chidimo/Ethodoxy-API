             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Eliphaz returns to the charge against Job, and describes the wretched state of the wicked.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               And Eliphaz the Themanite, answered, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Will a wise man answer as if he were speaking in the wind, and fill his stomach with burning heat?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Thou reprovest him by words, who is not equal to thee, and thou speakest that which is not good for thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               As much as is in thee,
               <u>
                thou hast made void fear
               </u>
               , and hast taken away prayers from before God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               For thy iniquity hath taught thy mouth, and thou imitatest the tongue of blasphemers.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [4]
               <u>
                "Thou hast made void fear"
               </u>
               : That is, cast off the fear of offending God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Thy own mouth shall condemn thee, and not I: and thy own lips shall answer thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Art thou the first man that was born, or wast thou made before the hills?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Hast thou heard God's counsel, and shall his wisdom be inferior to thee?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               What knowest thou that we are ignorant of? what dost thou understand that we know not?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               There are with us also aged and ancient men, much elder than thy fathers.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Is it a great matter that God should comfort thee? but thy wicked words hinder this.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Why doth thy heart elevate thee, and why dost thou stare with thy eyes, as if they were thinking great things?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Why doth thy spirit swell against God, to utter such words out of thy mouth?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               What is man that he should be without spot, and he that is born of a woman that he should appear just?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Behold among his saints none is unchangeable, and the heavens are not pure in his sight.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               How much more is man abominable, and unprofitable, who drinketh iniquity like water?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               I will shew thee, hear me: and I will tell thee what I have seen.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               <u>
                Wise men confess and hide not their fathers
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               To whom alone the earth was given, and no stranger hath passed among them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               The wicked man is proud all his days, and the number of the years of his tyranny is uncertain.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [18]
               <u>
                "Wise men confess and hide not their fathers"
               </u>
               : That is, the knowledge and documents they have received from their fathers they are not ashamed to own.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               The sound of dread is always in his ears: and when there is peace, he always suspecteth treason.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               He believeth not that he may return from darkness to light, looking round about for the sword on every side.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               When he moveth himself to seek bread, he knoweth that the day of darkness is ready at his hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Tribulation shall terrify him, and distress shall surround him, as a king that is prepared for the battle.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               For he hath stretched out his hand against God, and hath strengthened himself against the Almighty.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               He hath run against him with his neck raised up, and is armed with a fat neck.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Fatness hath covered his face, and the fat hangeth down on his sides.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               He hath dwelt in desolate cities, and in desert houses that are reduced into heaps.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               He shall not be enriched, neither shall his substance continue, neither shall he push his root in the earth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               He shall not depart out of darkness: the flame shall dry up his branches, and he shall be taken away by the breath of his own mouth.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               He shall not believe, being vainly deceived by error, that he may be redeemed with any price.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               Before his days be full he shall perish: and his hands shall wither away.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               He shall be blasted as a vine when its grapes are in the first flower, and as an olive tree that casteth its flower.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               For the congregation of the hypocrite is barren, and fire shall devour their tabernacles, who love to take bribes.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=15&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               He hath conceived sorrow, and hath brought forth iniquity, and his womb prepareth deceits.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

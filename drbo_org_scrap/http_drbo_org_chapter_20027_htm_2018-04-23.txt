             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job persists in asserting his own innocence, and that hypocrites will be punished in the end.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Job also added, taking up his parable, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               As God liveth, who hath taken away my judgment, and the Almighty, who hath brought my soul to bitterness,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               As long as breath remaineth in me, and the spirit of God in my nostrils,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               My lips shall not speak iniquity, neither shall my tongue contrive lying.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               God forbid that I should judge you to be just: till I die I will not depart from my innocence.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               My justification, which I have begun to hold, I will not forsake: for my heart doth not reprehend me in all my life.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Let my enemy be as the ungodly, and my adversary as the wicked one.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               For what is the hope of the hypocrite if through covetousness he take by violence, and God deliver not his soul?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Will God hear his cry, when distress shall come upon him?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Or can he delight himself in the Almighty, and call upon God at all times?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               I will teach you by the hand of God, what the Almighty hath, and I will not conceal it.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Behold you all know it, and why do you speak vain things without cause?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               This is the portion of a wicked man with God, and the inheritance of the violent, which they shall receive of the Almighty.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               If his sons be multiplied, they shall be for the sword, and his grandsons shall not be filled with bread.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               They that shall remain of him, shall be buried in death, and his widows shall not weep.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               If he shall heap together silver as earth, and prepare raiment as clay,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               He shall prepare indeed, but the just man shall be clothed with it: and the innocent shall divide the silver.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               He hath built his house as a moth, and as a keeper he hath made a booth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               The rich man when he shall sleep shall take away nothing with him: he shall open his eyes and find nothing.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Poverty like water shall take hold on him, a tempest shall oppress him in the night.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               A burning wind shall take him up, and carry him away, and as a whirlwind shall snatch him from his place.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               And he shall cast upon him, and shall not spare: out of his hand he would willingly flee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=27&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               He shall clasp his hands upon him, and shall hiss at him, beholding his place.
              </p>
             </td>
            </tr>

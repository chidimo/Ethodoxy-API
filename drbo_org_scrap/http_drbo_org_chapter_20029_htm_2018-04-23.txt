             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Job relates his former happiness, and the respect that all men shewed him.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Job also added, taking up his parable, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Who will grant me, that I might be according to the months past, according to the days in which God kept me?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               When his lamp shined over my head, and I walked by his light in darkness?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               As I was in the days of my youth, when God was secretly in my tabernacle?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               When the Almighty was with me: and my servants round about me?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               When I washed my feet with butter, and the rock poured me out rivers of oil?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               When I went out to the gate of the city, and in the street they prepared me a chair?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The young men saw me, and hid themselves: and the old men rose up and stood.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               The princes ceased to speak, and laid the finger on their mouth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               The rulers held their peace, and their tongue cleaved to their throat.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The ear that heard me blessed me, and the eye that saw me gave witness to me:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Because I had delivered the poor man that cried out; and the fatherless that had no helper.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               The blessing of him that was ready to perish came upon me, and I comforted the heart of the widow.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               I was clad with justice: and I clothed myself with my judgment, as with a robe and a diadem.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               I was an eye to the blind, and a foot to the lame.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               I was the father of the poor: and the cause which I knew not, I searched out most diligently.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               I broke the jaws of the wicked man, and out of his teeth I took away the prey.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               And I said: I shall die in my nest, and as a palm tree shall multiply my days.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               My root is opened beside the waters, and dew shall continue in my harvest.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               My glory shall always be renewed, and my bow in my hand shall be repaired.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               They that heard me, waited for my sentence, and being attentive held their peace at my counsel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               To my words they durst add nothing, and my speech dropped upon them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               They waited for me as for rain, and they opened their mouth as for a latter shower.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               If at any time I laughed on them, they believed not, and the light of my countenance fell not on earth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=29&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               If I had a mind to go to them, I sat first, and when I sat as a king, with his army standing about him, yet I was a comforter of them that mourned.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Eliu proceeds in setting forth the justice and power of God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Eliu also proceeded, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Suffer me a little, and I will shew thee: for I have yet somewhat to speak in God's behalf.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               I will repeat my knowledge from the beginning, and I will prove my Maker just.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               For indeed my words are without a lie, and perfect knowledge shall be proved to thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               God doth not cast away the mighty, whereas he himself also is mighty.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               But he saveth not the wicked, and he giveth judgment to the poor.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               He will not take away his eyes from the just, and he placeth kings on the throne for ever, and they are exalted.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               And if they shall be in chains, and be bound with the cords of poverty:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He shall shew them their works, and their wicked deeds, because they have been violent.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               He also shall open their ear, to correct them: and shall speak, that they may return from iniquity.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               If they shall hear and observe, they shall accomplish their days in good, and their years in glory.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               But if they hear not, they shall pass by the sword, and shall be consumed in folly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Dissemblers and crafty men prove the wrath of God, neither shall they cry when they are bound.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Their soul shall die in a storm, and their life among the effeminate.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               He shall deliver the poor out of his distress, and shall open his ear in affliction.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Therefore he shall set thee at large
               <u>
                out of the narrow mouth
               </u>
               , and which hath no foundation under it: and the rest of thy table shall be full of fatness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Thy cause hath been judged as that of the wicked, cause and judgment thou shalt recover.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Therefore let not anger overcome thee to oppress any man: neither let multitude of gifts turn thee aside.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Lay down thy greatness without tribulation, and all the mighty of strength.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Prolong not the night that people may come up for them.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [16]
               <u>
                "Out of the narrow mouth"
               </u>
               : That is, out of hell, whose entrance is narrow, and its depth bottomless; but figuratively meant here, that is, from his miseries and calamity to be restored to his former state of happiness.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Beware thou turn not aside to iniquity:
               <u>
                for this thou hast begun to follow after misery
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Behold, God is high in his strength, and none is like him among the lawgivers.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Who can search out his ways? or who can say to him: Thou has wrought iniquity?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Remember that thou knowest not his work, concerning which men have sung.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               All men see him, every one beholdeth afar off.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [21]
               <u>
                "For this thou hast begun to follow after misery"
               </u>
               : Eliu charges Job, that notwithstanding his misery, he does not fear God as he ought: but in his judgment, falls into iniquity.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               Behold, God is great, exceeding our knowledge: the number of his years is inestimable.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               He lifteth up the drops of rain, and poureth out showers like floods:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Which flow from the clouds that cover all above.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               If he will spread out clouds as his tent,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               And lighten with his light from above, he shall cover also the ends of the sea.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               For by these he judgeth people, and giveth food to many mortals.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               In his hands he hideth the light, and commandeth it to come again.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=36&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               He sheweth his friend concerning it, that it is his possession, and that he may come up to it.
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               God interposes and shews from the things he hath made, that man cannot comprehend his power and wisdom.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Then
               <u>
                the Lord
               </u>
               answered Job out of a whirlwind, and said:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Who is this that wrappeth up sentences in unskillful words?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Gird up thy loins like a man: I will ask thee, and answer thou me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Where wast thou when I laid up the foundations of the earth? tell me if thou hast understanding.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Who hath laid the measures thereof, if thou knowest? or who hath stretched the line upon it?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "The Lord"
               </u>
               : That is, an angel speaking in the name of the Lord.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Upon what are its bases grounded? or who laid the corner stone thereof,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               When the morning stars praised me together, and all the sons of God made a joyful melody?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Who shut up the sea with doors, when it broke forth as issuing out of the womb:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               When I made a cloud the garment thereof, and wrapped it in a mist as in swaddling bands?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               I set my bounds around it, and made it bars and doors:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And I said: Hitherto thou shalt come, and shalt go no further, and here thou shalt break thy swelling waves.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Didst thou since thy birth command the morning, and shew the dawning of the day its place?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And didst thou hold the extremities of the earth shaking them, and hast thou shaken the ungodly out of it?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               The seal shall be restored as clay, and shall stand as a garment:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               From the wicked their light shall be taken away, and the high arm shall be broken.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Hast thou entered into the depths of the sea, and walked in the lowest parts of the deep?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Have the gates of death been opened to thee, and hast thou seen the darksome doors?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Hast thou considered the breadth of the earth? tell me, if thou knowest all things?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Where is the way where light dwelleth, and where is the place of darkness:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               That thou mayst bring every thing to its own bounds, and understand the paths of the house thereof.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Didst thou know then that thou shouldst be born? and didst thou know the number of thy days?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Hast thou entered into the storehouses of the snow, or has thou beheld the treasures of the hail:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Which I have prepared for the time of the enemy, against the day of battle and war?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               By what way is the light spread, and heat divided upon the earth?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Who gave a course to violent showers, or a way for noisy thunder:
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               That it should rain on the earth without man in the wilderness, where no mortal dwelleth:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               That it should fill the desert and desolate land, and should bring forth green grass?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Who is the father of rain? or who begot the drops of dew?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               Out of whose womb came the ice; and the frost from heaven who hath gendered it?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               The waters are hardened like a stone, and the surface of the deep is congealed.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               Shalt thou be able to join together the shining stars the
               <u>
                Pleiades
               </u>
               , or canst thou stop the turning about of Arcturus?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               Canst thou bring forth the day star in its time, and make the evening star to rise upon the children of the earth?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               Dost thou know the order of heaven, and canst thou set down the reason thereof on the earth?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               Canst thou lift up thy voice to the clouds, that an abundance of waters may cover thee?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               Canst thou send lightnings, and will they go, and will they return and say to thee: Here we are?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [31]
               <u>
                "Pleiades"
               </u>
               : Hebrew, Cimah. A cluster of seven stars in the constellation Taurus or the Bull. Arcturus, a bright star in the constellation Bootes. The Hebrew name Cesil, is variously interpreted; by some, Orion; by others, the Great Bear is understood.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=36-#x">
                [36]
               </a>
               Who hath put wisdom in the heart of man? or who gave the cock
               <u>
                understanding
               </u>
               ?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=37-#x">
                [37]
               </a>
               Who can declare the order of the heavens, or who can make the harmony of heaven to sleep?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=38-#x">
                [38]
               </a>
               When was the dust poured on the earth, and the clods fastened together?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=39-#x">
                [39]
               </a>
               Wilt thou take the prey for the lioness, and satisfy the appetite of her whelps,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=40-#x">
                [40]
               </a>
               When they couch in the dens and lie in wait in holes?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [36]
               <u>
                "Understanding"
               </u>
               : That instinct by which he distinguishes the times of crowing in the night.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=20&amp;ch=38&amp;l=41-#x">
                [41]
               </a>
               Who provideth food for the raven, when her young ones cry to God, wandering about, because they have no meat?
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               A wise woman buildeth her house: but the foolish will pull down with her hands that also which is built.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               He that walketh in the right way, and feareth God, is despised by him that goeth by an infamous way.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               In the mouth of a fool is the rod of pride: but the lips of the wise preserve them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Where there are no oxen, the crib is empty: but where there is much corn, there the strength of the ox is manifest.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               A faithful witness will not lie: but a deceitful witness uttereth a lie.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               A scorner seeketh wisdom, and findeth it not: the learning of the wise is easy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Go against a foolish man, and he knoweth not the lips of prudence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The wisdom of a discreet man is to understand his way: and the imprudence of fools erreth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               A fool will laugh at sin, but among the just grace shall abide.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               The heart that knoweth the bitterness of his own soul, in his joy the stranger shall not intermeddle.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               The house of the wicked shall be destroyed: but the tabernacles of the just shall flourish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               There is a way which seemeth just to a man: but the ends thereof lead to death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Laughter shall be mingled with sorrow, and mourning taketh hold of the end of joy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               A fool shall be filled with his own ways, and the good man shall be above him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               The innocent believeth every word: the discreet man considereth his steps. No good shall come to the deceitful son: but the wise servant shall prosper in his dealings, and his way shall be made straight.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               A wise man feareth and declineth from evil: the fool leapeth over and is confident.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               The impatient man shall work folly: and the crafty man is hateful.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               The childish shall possess folly, and the prudent, shall look for knowledge.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               The evil shall fall down before the good: and the wicked before the gates of the just.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               The poor man shall be hateful even to his own neighbour: but the friends of the rich are many.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               He that despiseth his neighbour, sinneth: but he that sheweth mercy to the poor, shall be blessed. He that believeth in the Lord, loveth mercy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               They err that work evil: but mercy and truth prepare good things.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               In much work there shall be abundance: but where there are many words, there is oftentimes want.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               The crown of the wise is their riches: the folly of fools, imprudence.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               A faithful witness delivereth souls: and the double dealer uttereth lies.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               In the fear of the Lord is confidence of strength, and there shall be hope for his children.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               The fear of the Lord is a fountain of life, to decline from the ruin of death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               In the multitude of people is the dignity of the king: and in the small number of people the dishonour of the prince.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               He that is patient, is governed with much wisdom: but he that is impatient, exalteth his folly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               Soundness of heart is the life of the flesh: but envy is the rottenness of the bones.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               He that oppresseth the poor, upbraideth his Maker: but he that hath pity on the poor, honoureth him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               The wicked man shall be driven out in his wickedness: but the just hath hope in his death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               In the heart of the prudent resteth wisdom, and it shall instruct all the ignorant.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               Justice exalteth a nation: but sin maketh nations miserable.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=14&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               A wise servant is acceptable to the king: he that is good for nothing shall feel his anger.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

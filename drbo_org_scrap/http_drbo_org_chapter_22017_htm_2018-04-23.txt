             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Better is a dry morsel with joy, than a house full of victims with strife.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               A wise servant shall rule over foolish sons, and shall divide the inheritance among the brethren.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               As silver is tried by fire, and gold in the furnace: so the Lord trieth the hearts.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               The evil man obeyeth an unjust tongue: and the deceitful hearkeneth to lying lips.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               He that despiseth the poor, reproacheth his Maker; and he that rejoiceth at another man's ruin, shall not be unpunished.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Children's children are the crown of old men: and the glory of children are their fathers.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Eloquent words do not become a fool, nor lying lips a prince.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The expectation of him that expecteth, is a most acceptable jewel: whithersoever he turneth himself, he understandeth wisely.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He that concealeth a transgression. seeketh friendships: he that repeateth it again, separateth friends.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               A reproof availeth more with a wise man, than a hundred stripes with a fool.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               An evil man always seeketh quarrels: but a cruel angel shall be sent against him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               It is better to meet a bear robbed of her whelps, than a fool trusting in his own folly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               He that rendereth evil for good, evil shall not depart from his house.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               The beginning of quarrels is as when one letteth out water: before he suffereth reproach he forsaketh judgment.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               He that justifieth the wicked, and he that condemneth the just, both are abominable before God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               What doth it avail a fool to have riches, seeing he cannot buy wisdom? He that maketh his house high, seeketh a downfall: and he that refuseth to learn, shall fall into evils.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               He that is a friend loveth at all times: and a brother is proved in distress.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               A foolish man will clap hands, when he is surety for his friend.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               He that studieth discords, loveth quarrels: and he that exalteth his door, seeketh ruin.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               He that is of a perverse heart, shall not find good: and he that perverteth his tongue, shall fall into evil.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               A fool is born to his own disgrace: and even his father shall not rejoice in a fool.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               A joyful mind maketh age flourishing: a sorrowful spirit drieth up the bones.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               The wicked man taketh gifts out of the bosom, that he may pervert the paths of judgment.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Wisdom shineth in the face of the wise: the eyes of fools are in the ends of the earth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               A foolish son is the anger of the father: and the sorrow of the mother that bore him.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               It is no good thing to do hurt to the just: nor to strike the prince, who judgeth right.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               He that setteth bounds to his words. is knowing and wise: and the man of understanding is of a precious spirit.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=17&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Even a fool, if he will hold his peace shall be counted wise: and if he close his lips, a man of understanding.
              </p>
             </td>
            </tr>

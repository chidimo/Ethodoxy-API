             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               As the divisions of waters, so the heart of the king is in the hand of the Lord: whithersoever he will he shall turn it.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Every way of a man seemeth right to himself: but the Lord weigheth the hearts.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               To do mercy and judgment, pleaseth the Lord more than victims.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Haughtiness of the eyes is the enlarging of the heart: the lamp of the wicked is sin.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               The thoughts of the industrious always bring forth abundance: but every sluggard is always in want.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               He that gathereth treasures by a lying tongue, is vain and foolish, and shall stumble upon the snares of death.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               The robberies of the wicked shall be their downfall, because they would not do judgment.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               The perverse way of a man is strange: but as for him that is pure, his work is right.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               It is better to sit in a corner of the housetop, than with a brawling women, and in a common house.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               The soul of the wicked desireth evil, he will not have pity on his neighbour.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               When a pestilent man is punished, the little one will be wiser: and if he follow the wise, he will receive knowledge.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               The just considereth seriously the house of the wicked, that he may withdraw the wicked from evil.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               He that stoppeth his ear against the cry of the poor, shall also cry himself and shall not be heard.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               A secret present quencheth anger: and a gift in the bosom the greatest wrath.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               It is joy to the just to do judgment: and dread to them that work iniquity.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               A man that shall wander out of the way of doctrine, shall abide in the company of the giants.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               He that loveth good cheer, shall be in want: he that loveth wine, and fat things, shall not be rich.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               The wicked is delivered up for the just: and the unjust for the righteous.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               It is better to dwell in a wilderness, than with a quarrelsome and passionate woman.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               There is a treasure to be desired, and oil in the dwelling of the just: and the foolish man shall spend it.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               He that followeth justice and mercy, shall find life, justice, and glory.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               The wise man hath scaled the city of the strong, and hath cast down the strength of the confidence thereof.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               He that keepeth his mouth and his tongue, keepeth his soul from distress.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               The proud and the arrogant is called ignorant, who in anger worketh pride.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Desires kill the slothful: for his hands have refused to work at all.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               He longeth and desireth all the day: but he that is just, will give, and will not cease.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               The sacrifices of the wicked are abominable, because they are offered of wickedness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               A lying witness shall perish: an obedient man shall speak of victory.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               The wicked man impudently hardeneth his face: but he that is righteous, correcteth his way.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               There is no wisdom, there is no prudence, there is no counsel against the Lord.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=21&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               The horse is prepared for the day of battle: but the Lord giveth safety.
              </p>

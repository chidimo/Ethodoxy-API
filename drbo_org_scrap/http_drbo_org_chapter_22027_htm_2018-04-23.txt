             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Boast not for tomorrow, for thou knowest not what the day to come may bring forth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Let another praise thee, and not thy own mouth: a stranger, and not thy own lips.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               A stone is heavy, and sand weighty: but the anger of a fool is heavier than them both.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Anger hath no mercy, nor fury when it breaketh forth: and who can bear the violence of one provoked?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Open rebuke is better than hidden love.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Better are the wounds of a friend, than the deceitful kisses of an enemy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               A soul that is full shall tread upon the honeycomb: and a soul that is hungry shall take even bitter for sweet.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               As a bird that wandereth from her nest, so is a man that leaveth his place.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Ointment and perfumes rejoice the heart: and the good counsels of a friend are sweet to the soul.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Thy own friend, and thy father's friend forsake not: and go not into thy brother's house in the day of thy affliction. Better is a neighbour that is near, than a brother afar off.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Study wisdom, my son, and make my heart joyful, that thou mayst give an answer to him that reproacheth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               The prudent man seeing evil hideth himself: little ones passing on have suffered losses.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Take away his garment that hath been surety for a stranger: and take from him a pledge for strangers.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               He that blesseth his neighbour with a loud voice, rising in the night, shall be like to him that curseth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Roofs dropping through in a cold day, and a contentious woman are alike.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               He that retaineth her, is as he that would hold the wind, and shall call in the oil of his right hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Iron sharpeneth iron, so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               He that keepeth the fig tree, shall eat the fruit thereof: and he that is the keeper of his master, shall be glorified.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               As the faces of them that look therein, shine in the water, so-the hearts of men are laid open to the wise.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Hell and destruction are never filled: so the eyes of men are never satisfied.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               As silver is tried in the fining-pot and gold in the furnace: so a man is tried by the mouth of him that praiseth. The heart of the wicked seeketh after evils, but the righteous heart seeketh after knowledge.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Though thou shouldst bray a fool in the mortar, as when a pestle striketh upon sodden barley, his folly would not be taken from him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Be diligent to know the countenance of thy cattle, and consider thy own flocks:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               For thou shalt not always have power: but a crown shall be given to generation and generation.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               The meadows are open, and the green herbs have appeared, and the hay is gathered out of the mountains.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               Lambs are for thy clothing: and kids for the price of the field.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=22&amp;ch=27&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Let the milk of the goats be enough for thy food, and for the necessities of thy house, and for maintenance for thy handmaids.
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               (No prolog)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Aleph. I am the man that see my poverty by the rod of his indignation.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Aleph. He hath led me, and brought me into darkness, and not into light.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Aleph. Only against me he hath turned, and turned again his hand all the day.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Beth. My skin and my flesh he hath made old, he hath broken my bones.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Beth. He hath built round about me, and he hath compassed me with gall and labour.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Beth. He hath set me in dark places as those that are dead for ever.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Ghimel. He hath built against me round about, that I may not get out: he hath made my fetters heavy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Ghimel. Yea, and when I cry, and entreat, he hath shut out my prayer.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Ghimel. He hath shut up my ways with square stones, he hath turned my paths upside down.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Daleth. He is become to me as a bear lying in wait: as a lion in secret places.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Daleth. He hath turned aside my paths, and hath broken me in pieces, he hath made me desolate.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Daleth. He hath bent his bow, and set me as a mark for his arrows.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               He. He hath shot into my reins the daughters of his quiver.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               He. I am made a derision to all my people, their song all the day long.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               He. He hath filled me with bitterness, he hath inebriated me with wormwood.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Vau. And he hath broken my teeth one by one, he hath fed me with ashes.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               Vau. And my soul is removed far off from peace, I have forgotten good things.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Vau. And I said: My end and my hope is perished from the Lord.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Zain. Remember my poverty, and transgression, the wormwood, and the gall.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Zain. I will be mindful and remember, and my soul shall languish within me.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Zain. These things I shall think over in my heart, therefore will I hope.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               Heth. The mercies of the Lord that we are not consumed: because his commiserations have not failed.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Heth. They are new every morning, great is thy faithfulness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               Heth. The Lord is my portion, said my soul: therefore will I wait for him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               Teth. The Lord is good to them that hope in him, to the soul that seeketh him.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               Teth. It is good to wait with silence for the salvation of God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               Teth. It is good for a man, when he hath borne the yoke from his youth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=28-#x">
                [28]
               </a>
               Jod. He shall sit solitary, and hold his peace: because he hath taken it up upon himself.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=29-#x">
                [29]
               </a>
               Jod. He shall put his mouth in the dust, if so be there may be hope.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=30-#x">
                [30]
               </a>
               Jod. He shall give his cheek to him that striketh him, he shall be filled with reproaches.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=31-#x">
                [31]
               </a>
               Caph. For the Lord will not cast off for ever.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=32-#x">
                [32]
               </a>
               Caph. For if he hath cast off, he will also have mercy, according to the multitude of his mercies.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=33-#x">
                [33]
               </a>
               Caph. For he hath not willingly afflicted, nor cast off the children of men.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=34-#x">
                [34]
               </a>
               Lamed. To crush under his feet all the prisoners of the land,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=35-#x">
                [35]
               </a>
               Lamed. To turn aside the judgment of a man before the face of the most High,
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=36-#x">
                [36]
               </a>
               Lamed. To destroy a man wrongfully in his judgment, the Lord hath not approved.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=37-#x">
                [37]
               </a>
               Mem. Who is he that hath commanded a thing to be done, when the Lord commandeth it not?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=38-#x">
                [38]
               </a>
               Mem. Shall not both evil and good proceed out of the mouth of the Highest?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=39-#x">
                [39]
               </a>
               Mem. Why hath a living man murmured, man suffering for his sins?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=40-#x">
                [40]
               </a>
               Nun. Let us search our ways, and seek, and return to the Lord.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=41-#x">
                [41]
               </a>
               Nun. Let us lift up our hearts with our hands to the Lord in the heavens.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=42-#x">
                [42]
               </a>
               Nun. We have done wickedly, and provoked thee to wrath: therefore thou art inexorable.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=43-#x">
                [43]
               </a>
               Samech. Thou hast covered in thy wrath, and hast struck us: thou hast killed and hast not spared.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=44-#x">
                [44]
               </a>
               Samech. Thou hast set a cloud before thee, that our prayer may not pass through.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=45-#x">
                [45]
               </a>
               Samech. Thou hast made me as an outcast, and refuse in the midst of the people.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=46-#x">
                [46]
               </a>
               Phe. All our enemies have opened their mouths against us.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=47-#x">
                [47]
               </a>
               Phe. Prophecy is become to us a fear, and a snare, and destruction.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=48-#x">
                [48]
               </a>
               Phe. My eye hath run down with streams of water, for the destruction of the daughter of my people.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=49-#x">
                [49]
               </a>
               Ain. My eye is afflicted, and hath not been quiet, because there was no rest:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=50-#x">
                [50]
               </a>
               Ain. Till the Lord regarded and looked down from the heavens.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=51-#x">
                [51]
               </a>
               Ain. My eye hath wasted my soul because of all the daughters of my city.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=52-#x">
                [52]
               </a>
               Sade. My enemies have chased me and caught me like a bird, without cause.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=53-#x">
                [53]
               </a>
               Sade. My life is fallen into the pit, and they have laid a stone over me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=54-#x">
                [54]
               </a>
               Sade. Waters have flowed over my head: I said: I am cut off.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=55-#x">
                [55]
               </a>
               Coph. I have called upon thy name, O Lord, from the lowest pit.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=56-#x">
                [56]
               </a>
               Coph. Thou hast heard my voice: turn not away thy ear from my sighs, and cries.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=57-#x">
                [57]
               </a>
               Coph. Thou drewest near in the day, when I called upon thee, thou saidst: Fear not.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=58-#x">
                [58]
               </a>
               Res. Thou hast judged, O Lord, the cause of my soul, thou the Redeemer of my life.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=59-#x">
                [59]
               </a>
               Res. Thou hast seen, O Lord, their iniquity against me: judge thou my judgment.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=60-#x">
                [60]
               </a>
               Res. Thou hast seen all their fury, and all their thoughts against me.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=61-#x">
                [61]
               </a>
               Sin. Thou hast heard their reproach, O Lord, all their imaginations against me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=62-#x">
                [62]
               </a>
               Sin. The lips of them that rise up against me: and their devices against me all the day.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=63-#x">
                [63]
               </a>
               Sin. Behold their sitting down, and their rising up, I am their song.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=64-#x">
                [64]
               </a>
               Thau. Thou shalt render them a recompense, O Lord, according to the works of their hands.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=65-#x">
                [65]
               </a>
               Thau. Thou shalt give them a buckler of heart, thy labour.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=29&amp;ch=3&amp;l=66-#x">
                [66]
               </a>
               Thau. Thou shalt persecute them in anger, and shalt destroy them from under the heavens, O Lord.
              </p>

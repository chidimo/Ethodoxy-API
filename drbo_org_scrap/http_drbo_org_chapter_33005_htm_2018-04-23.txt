             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               God's threats against the priests, the people, and princes of Israel, for their idolatry.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Hear ye this,
               <u>
                O priests
               </u>
               , and hearken, O ye house of Israel, and give ear, O house of the king: for there is a judgment against you, because you have been a snare to them whom you should have watched over, and a net spread upon Thabor.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And you have turned aside victims into the depth: and I am, the teacher of them all.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               I know Ephraim, and Israel is not hid from me: for now Ephraim hath committed fornication, Israel is defiled.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               They will not set their thoughts to return to their God: for the spirit of fornication is in the midst of them, and they have not known the Lord.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And the pride of Israel shall answer in his face: and Israel and Ephraim shall fall in their iniquity, Juda also shall fall with them.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "O priests"
               </u>
               : What is said of priests in this prophecy is chiefly understood of the priests of the kingdom of Israel; who were not true priests of the race of Aaron; but served the calves at Bethel and Dan.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               With their flocks, and with their herds, they shall go to seek the Lord, and shall not find him: he is withdrawn from them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               They have transgressed against the Lord, for they have begotten
               <u>
                children that are strangers
               </u>
               : now shall a month devour them with their portions.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Blow ye the cornet in Gabaa, the trumpet in Rama: howl ye in Bethaven, behind thy back, O Benjamin.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               Ephraim shall be in desolation in the day of rebuke: among the tribes of Israel I have shewn that which shall surely be.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               The princes of Juda are become
               <u>
                as they that take up the bound
               </u>
               : I will pour out my wrath upon them like water.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [7]
               <u>
                "Children that are strangers"
               </u>
               : That is, aliens from God: and therefore they are threatened with speedy destruction.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [10]
               <u>
                "As they that take up the bound"
               </u>
               : That is, they that remove the boundary, encroaching on the property of their neighbours: figuratively: going beyond the boundary of the laws of God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Ephraim is under oppression, and broken in judgment: because he began to go after filthiness.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               And I will be like a moth to Ephraim: and like rottenness to the house of Juda.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               And Ephraim saw his sickness, and Juda his band: and Ephraim went to the Assyrian, and sent to the avenging king: and he shall not be able to heal you, neither shall he be able to take off the band from you.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               For I will be like a lioness to Ephraim, and like a lion's whelp to the house of Juda: I, I will catch, and go: I will take away, and there is none that can rescue.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=5&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               I will go and return to my place: until you are consumed, and seek my face.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

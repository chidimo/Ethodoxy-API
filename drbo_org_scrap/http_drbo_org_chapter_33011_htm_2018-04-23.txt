             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               God proceeds in threatening Israel for their ingratitude: yet he will not utterly destroy them.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               As the morning passeth, so hath the king of Israel passed away. Because Israel was a child, and I loved him: and
               <u>
                I called my son
               </u>
               out of Egypt.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               As
               <u>
                they called
               </u>
               them, they went away from before their face: they offered victims to Baalim, and sacrificed to idols.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And I was like a foster father to Ephraim, I carried them in my arms: and they knew not that I healed them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               I will draw them with the cords of Adam, with the bands of love: and I will be to them as one that taketh off the yoke on their jaws: and I put his meat to him that he might eat.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               He shall not return into the land of Egypt, but the Assyrian shall be his king: because they would not be converted.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "I called my son"
               </u>
               : Viz., Israel. But as the calling of Israel out of Egypt, was a figure of the calling of Christ from thence; therefore this text is also applicable to Christ, as we learn from Matthew 2. 15.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [2]
               <u>
                "They called"
               </u>
               : Viz., Moses and Aaron called; but they went away after other gods and would not hear.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               The sword hath begun in his cities, and it shall consume his chosen men, and shall devour their heads.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               And my people shall long for my return: but a yoke shall be put upon them together, which shall not be taken off.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               How shall I deal with thee, O Ephraim, shall I protect thee, O Israel? how shall I make thee as
               <u>
                Adama
               </u>
               , shall I set thee as Seboim? my heart is turned within me, my repentance is stirred up.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               I will not execute the fierceness of my wrath: I will not return to destroy Ephraim: because I am God, and not man: the holy one in the midst of thee, and I will not enter into the city.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               They shall walk after the Lord, he shall roar as a lion: because he shall roar, and the children of the sea shall fear.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "Adama"
               </u>
               : Adama and Seboim were two cities in the neighbourhood of Sodom: and underwent the like destruction.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And they shall fly away like a bird out of Egypt, and like a dove out of the land of the Assyrians: and I will place them in their own houses, saith the Lord.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=33&amp;ch=11&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Ephraim hath compassed me about with denials, and the house of Israel with deceit: but Juda went down as a witness with God, and is faithful with the saints.
              </p>

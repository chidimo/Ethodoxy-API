             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               A lamentation for Israel: an exhortation to return to God.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Hear ye this word, which I take up concerning you for a lamentation. The house of Israel is fallen, and it shall rise no more.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               The virgin of Israel is cast down upon her land, there is none to raise her up.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               For thus saith the Lord God: The city, out of which came forth a thousand, there shall be left in it a hundred: and out of which there came a hundred, there shall be left in it ten, in the house of Israel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               For thus saith the Lord to the house of Israel: Seek ye me, and you shall live.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               <u>
                But seek not Bethel
               </u>
               , and go not into Galgal, neither shall you pass over to Bersabee: for Galgal shall go into captivity, and Bethel shall be unprofitable.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [5]
               <u>
                "But seek not Bethel"
               </u>
               : Bethel, Galgal, Bersabee: the places where they worshipped their idols.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Seek ye the Lord, and live: lest the house of Joseph be burnt with fire, and it shall devour, and there shall be none to quench Bethel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               You that turn judgment into wormwood, and forsake justice in the land,
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Seek him that maketh
               <u>
                Arcturus, and Orion
               </u>
               , and that turneth darkness into morning, and that changeth day into night: that calleth the waters of the sea, and poureth them out upon the face of the earth: The Lord is his name.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               He that
               <u>
                with a smile
               </u>
               bringeth destruction upon the strong, and waste upon the mighty.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               They have hated him that rebuketh in the gate: and have abhorred him that speaketh perfectly.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "Arcturus and Orion"
               </u>
               : Arcturus is a bright star in the north: Orion a beautiful constellation in the south.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [9]
               <u>
                "With a smile"
               </u>
               : That is, with all ease, and without making any effort.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Therefore because you robbed the poor, and took the choice prey from him: you shall build houses with square stone, and shall not dwell in them: you shall plant most delightful vineyards, and shall not drink the wine of them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               Because I know your manifold crimes, and your grievous sins: enemies of the just, taking bribes, and oppressing the poor in the gate.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Therefore the prudent shall keep silence at that time, for it is an evil time.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Seek ye good, and not evil, that you may live: and the Lord the God of hosts will be with you, as you have said.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Hate evil, and love good, and establish judgment in the gate: it may be the Lord the God of hosts may have mercy on the remnant of Joseph.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               Therefore thus saith the Lord the God of hosts the sovereign Lord: In every street there shall be wailing: and in all places that are without, they shall say: Alas, alas! and they shall call the husbandman to mourning, and such as are skillful in lamentation to lament.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               And in all vineyards there shall be wailing: because I will pass through in the midst of thee, saith the Lord.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               Woe to them that desire the day of the Lord: to what end is it for you? the day of the Lord is darkness, and not light.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               As if a man should flee from the face of a lion, and a bear should meet him: or enter into the house, and lean with his hand upon the wall, and a serpent should bite him.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Shall not the day of the Lord be darkness, and not light: and obscurity, and no brightness in it?
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               I hate, and have rejected your festivities: and I will not receive the odour of your assemblies.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               And if you offer me holocausts, and your gifts, I will not receive them: neither will I regard the vows of your fat beasts.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=23-#x">
                [23]
               </a>
               Take away from me the tumult of thy songs: and I will not hear the canticles of thy harp.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=24-#x">
                [24]
               </a>
               But judgment shall be revealed as water, and justice as a mighty torrent.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=25-#x">
                [25]
               </a>
               <u>
                Did you offer
               </u>
               victims and sacrifices to me in the desert for forty years, O house of Israel?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [25]
               <u>
                "Did you offer"
               </u>
               : Except the sacrifices that were offered at the first, in the dedication of the tabernacle, the Israelites offered no sacrifices in the desert.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=26-#x">
                [26]
               </a>
               But you carried
               <u>
                a tabernacle
               </u>
               for your Moloch, and the image of your idols, the star of your god, which you made to yourselves.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=35&amp;ch=5&amp;l=27-#x">
                [27]
               </a>
               And I will cause you to go into captivity beyond Damascus, saith the Lord, the God of hosts is his name.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [26]
               <u>
                "A tabernacle"
               </u>
               : All this alludes to the idolatry which they committed, when they were drawn away by the daughters of Moab to the worship of their gods. Num. 25.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

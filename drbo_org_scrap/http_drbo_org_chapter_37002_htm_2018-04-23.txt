             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               Jonas is swallowed up by a great fish: he prayeth with confidence in God; and the fish casteth him out on the dry land.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Now the Lord prepared a great fish to swallow up Jonas: and Jonas was in the belly of the fish three days and three nights.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And Jonas prayed to the Lord his God out of the belly of the fish.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And he said: I cried out of my affliction to the Lord, and he heard me: I cried out of the belly of hell, and thou hast heard my voice.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And thou hast cast me forth into the deep in the heart of the sea, and a flood hath compassed me: all thy billows, and thy waves have passed over me.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And I said: I am cast away out of the sight of thy eyes: but yet I shall see thy holy temple again.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               The waters compassed me about even to the soul: the deep hath closed me round about, the sea hath covered my head.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               I went down to the lowest parts of the mountains: the bars of the earth have shut me up for ever: and thou wilt bring up my life from corruption, O Lord my God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               When my soul was in distress within me, I remembered the Lord: that my prayer may come to thee, unto thy holy temple.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               They that are vain observe vanities, forsake their own mercy.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               But I with the voice of praise will sacrifice to thee: I will pay whatsoever I have vowed for my salvation to the Lord.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=37&amp;ch=2&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And the Lord
               <u>
                spoke to the fish
               </u>
               : and it vomited out Jonas upon the dry land.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [11]
               <u>
                "Spoke to the fish"
               </u>
               : God's speaking to the fish, was nothing else but his will, which all things obey.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

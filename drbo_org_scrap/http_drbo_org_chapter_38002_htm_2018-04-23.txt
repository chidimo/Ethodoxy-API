             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               The Israelites by their crying injustices provoke God to punish them. He shall at last restore Jacob.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               Woe to you that devise that which is unprofitable, and work evil in your beds: in the morning light they execute it, because their hand is against God.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And they have coveted fields, and taken them by violence, and houses they have forcibly taken away: and oppressed a man and his house, a man and his inheritance.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               Therefore thus saith the Lord: Behold, I devise an evil against this family: from which you shall not withdraw your necks, and you shall not walk haughtily, for this is a very evil time.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               In that day a parable shall be taken up upon you, and a song shall be sung with melody by them that say: We are laid waste and spoiled: the portion of my people is changed:
               <u>
                how shall he depart
               </u>
               from me, whereas he is returning that will divide our land?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               Therefore
               <u>
                thou shalt have none
               </u>
               that shall cast the cord of a lot in the assembly of the Lord.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [4]
               <u>
                "How shall he depart"
               </u>
               : How do you pretend to say that the Assyrian is departing; when indeed he is coming to divide our lands amongst his subjects?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [5]
               <u>
                "Thou shalt have none"
               </u>
               : Thou shalt have no longer any lot or inheritance in the land of the people of the Lord.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               Speak ye not, saying:
               <u>
                It shall not drop
               </u>
               upon these, confusion shall not take them.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               The house of Jacob saith: Is the spirit of the Lord straitened, or are these his thoughts? Are not my words good to him that walketh uprightly?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               But my people, on the contrary, are risen up as an enemy:
               <u>
                you have taken away
               </u>
               the cloak off from the coat: and them that passed harmless you have turned to war.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               <u>
                You have cast out
               </u>
               the women of my people from their houses, in which they took delight: you have taken my praise for ever from their children.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Arise ye, and depart, for there is no rest here for you. For that uncleanness of the land, it shall be corrupted with a grievous corruption.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [6]
               <u>
                "It shall not drop"
               </u>
               : That is, the prophecy shall not come upon these. Such were the sentiments of the people that were unwilling to believe the threats of the prophets.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "You have taken away"
               </u>
               : You have even stripped people of their necessary garments: and have treated such as were innocently passing on the way, as if they were at war with you.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [9]
               <u>
                "You have cast out"
               </u>
               : either by depriving them of their houses: or, by your crimes, given occasion to their being carried away captives, and their children, by that means, never learning to praise the Lord.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               <u>
                Would God
               </u>
               I were not a man that hath the spirit, and that I rather spoke a lie: I will let drop to thee of wine, and of drunkenness: and it shall be this people upon whom it shall drop.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               I will assemble and gather together all of thee, O Jacob: I will bring together the remnant of Israel, I will put them together as a flock in the fold, as the sheep in the midst of the sheepcotes, they shall make a tumult by reason of the multitude of men.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=38&amp;ch=2&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               For he shall go up that shall open the way before them: they shall divide, and pass through the gate, and shall come in by it: and their king shall pass before them, and the Lord at the head of them.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [11]
               <u>
                "Would God"
               </u>
               : The prophet could have wished, out of his love to his people, that he might be deceived in denouncing to them these evils that were to fall upon them: but by conforming himself to the will of God, he declares to them, that he is sent to prophesy, literally to let drop upon them, the wine of God's indignation, with which they should be made drunk; that is, stupified and cast down.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

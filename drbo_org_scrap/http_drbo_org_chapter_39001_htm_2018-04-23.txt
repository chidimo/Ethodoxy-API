           <td class="textarea" valign="top">
            <p class="desc">
             The majesty of God, his goodness to his people, and severity to his enemies.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=1-#x">
              [1]
             </a>
             The burden of Ninive. The book of the vision of Nahum the Elcesite.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=2-#x">
              [2]
             </a>
             The Lord is a jealous God, and a revenger: the Lord is a revenger, and hath wrath: the Lord taketh vengeance on his adversaries, and he is angry with his enemies.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=3-#x">
              [3]
             </a>
             The Lord is patient, and great in power, and will not cleanse and acquit the guilty. The Lord's ways are in a tempest, and a whirlwind, and clouds are the dust of his feet.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=4-#x">
              [4]
             </a>
             He rebuketh the sea, and drieth it up: and bringeth all the rivers to be a desert. Basan languisheth and Carmel: and the flower of Libanus fadeth away.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=5-#x">
              [5]
             </a>
             The mountains tremble at him, and the hills are made desolate: and the earth hath quaked at his presence, and the world, and all that dwell therein.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=6-#x">
              [6]
             </a>
             Who can stand before the face of his indignation? and who shall resist in the fierceness of his anger? his indignation is poured out like fire: and the rocks are melted by him.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=7-#x">
              [7]
             </a>
             The Lord is good and giveth strength in the day of trouble: and knoweth them that hope in him.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=8-#x">
              [8]
             </a>
             But with a flood that passeth by, he will make an utter end
             <u>
              of the place thereof
             </u>
             : and darkness shall pursue his enemies.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=9-#x">
              [9]
             </a>
             What do ye devise against the Lord? he will make an utter end: there shall not rise a double affliction.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=10-#x">
              [10]
             </a>
             For as thorns embrace one another: so while they are feasting and drinking together, they shall be consumed as stubble that is fully dry.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [8]
             <u>
              "Of the place thereof"
             </u>
             : Viz., of Ninive.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=11-#x">
              [11]
             </a>
             Out of thee
             <u>
              shall come forth one
             </u>
             that imagineth evil against the Lord, contriving treachery in his mind.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=12-#x">
              [12]
             </a>
             Thus saith the Lord:
             <u>
              Though they were perfect
             </u>
             : and many of them so, yet thus shall they be cut off, and he shall pass: I have afflicted thee, and I will afflict thee no more.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=13-#x">
              [13]
             </a>
             And now I will break in pieces his rod with which he struck thy back, and I will burst thy bonds asunder.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=14-#x">
              [14]
             </a>
             And the Lord
             <u>
              will give a commandment
             </u>
             concerning thee, that no more of thy name shall be sown: I will destroy the graven and molten thing out of the house of thy God, I will make it thy grave, for thou art disgraced.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=39&amp;ch=1&amp;l=15-#x">
              [15]
             </a>
             Behold upon the mountains the feet of him that bringeth good tidings, and that preacheth peace: O Juda, keep thy festivals, and pay thy vows: for
             <u>
              Belial
             </u>
             shall no more pass through thee again, he is utterly cut off.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [11]
             <u>
              "Shall come forth one"
             </u>
             : Some understand this of Sennacherib. But as his attempt against the people seems to have been prior to the prophecy of Nahum, we may better understand it of Holofernes.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [12]
             <u>
              "Though they were perfect"
             </u>
             : That is, however strong or numerous their forces may be, they shall be cut off; and their prince or leader shall pass away and disappear.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [14]
             <u>
              "Will give a commandment"
             </u>
             : That is, a decree, concerning thee, O king of Ninive, thy seed shall fail, etc.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [15]
             <u>
              "Belial"
             </u>
             : The wicked one, viz., the Assyrian.
            </p>

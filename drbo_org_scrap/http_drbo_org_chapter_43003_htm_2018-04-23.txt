             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               In a vision Satan appeareth accusing the high priest. He is cleansed from his sins. Christ is promised, and great fruit from his passion.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               And the Lord shewed me
               <u>
                Jesus
               </u>
               the high priest standing before the angel of the Lord: and Satan stood on his right hand to be his adversary.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And the Lord said to Satan: The Lord rebuke thee, O Satan: and the Lord that chose Jerusalem rebuke thee: Is not this a brand plucked out of the fire?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And Jesus was clothed
               <u>
                with filthy garments
               </u>
               : and he stood before the face of the angel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               Who answered, and said to them that stood before him, saying: Take away the filthy garments from him. And he said to him: Behold I have taken away thy iniquity, and have clothed thee with change of garments.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And he said: Put a clean mitre upon his head: and they put a clean mitre upon his head, and clothed him with garments, and the angel of the Lord stood.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "Jesus"
               </u>
               : Alias, Josue, the son of Josedec, the high priest of that time.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [3]
               <u>
                "With filthy garments"
               </u>
               : Negligences and sins.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               And the angel of the Lord protested to Jesus, saying:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               Thus saith the Lord of hosts: If thou wilt walk in my ways, and keep my charge, thou also shalt judge my house, and shalt keep my courts, and
               <u>
                I will give thee
               </u>
               some of them that are now present here to walk with thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               Hear, O Jesus thou high priest, thou and thy friends that dwell before thee, for they are
               <u>
                portending men
               </u>
               : for behold I WILL BRING
               <u>
                MY SERVANT THE ORIENT
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               For behold
               <u>
                the stone
               </u>
               that I have laid before Jesus: upon one stone there are
               <u>
                seven eyes
               </u>
               : behold I will grave the graving thereof, saith the Lord of hosts: and I will take away the iniquity of that land in
               <u>
                one day
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=3&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               In that day, saith the Lord of hosts, every man shall call his friend under the vine and under the fig tree.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [7]
               <u>
                "I will give thee"
               </u>
               : Angels to attend and assist thee.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "Portending men"
               </u>
               : That is, men, who by words and actions are to foreshew wonders that are to come.-- Ibid.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [8]
               <u>
                "My servant the Orient"
               </u>
               : Christ, who according to his humanity is the servant of God, is called the Orient from his rising like the sun in the east to enlighten the world.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [9]
               <u>
                "The stone"
               </u>
               : Another emblem of Christ, the rock, foundation, and corner stone of his church.-- Ibid.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [9]
               <u>
                "Seven eyes"
               </u>
               : The manifold providence of Christ over his church, or the seven gifts of the spirit of God.-- Ibid.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [9]
               <u>
                "One day"
               </u>
               : Viz., the day of the passion of Christ, the source of all our good: when this precious stone shall be graved, that is, cut and pierced, with whips, thorns, nails, and spear.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               The vision of the flying volume, and of the woman in the vessel.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               And I turned and lifted up my eyes: and I saw, and behold
               <u>
                a volume
               </u>
               flying.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               And he said to me: What seest thou? And I said: I see a volume flying: the length thereof is twenty cubits, and the breadth thereof ten cubits.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And he said to me: This is the curse that goeth forth over the face of the earth: for every thief shall be judged as is there written: and every one that sweareth in like manner shall be judged by it.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               I will bring it forth, saith the Lord of hosts: and it shall come to the house of the thief, and to the house of him that sweareth falsely by my name: and it shall remain in the midst of his house, and shall consume it, with the timber thereof, and the stones thereof.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And the angel went forth that spoke in me, and he said to me: Lift up thy eyes, and see what this is, that goeth forth.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "A volume"
               </u>
               : That is, a parchment, according to the form of the ancient books, which, from being rolled up, were called volumes.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               And I said: What is it? And he said: This is a vessel going forth. And he said:
               <u>
                This is their eye
               </u>
               in all the earth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               And behold a talent of lead was carried, and behold a woman sitting in the midst of the vessel.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               And he said: This is wickedness. And he cast her into the midst of the vessel, and cast the weight of lead upon the mouth thereof.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               And I lifted up my eyes and looked: and behold there came out two women, and wind was in their wings, and they had wings like the wings of a kite: and they lifted up the vessel between the earth and the heaven.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               And I said to the angel that spoke in me: Whither do these carry the vessel?
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [6]
               <u>
                "This is their eye"
               </u>
               : This is what they fix their eye upon: or this is a resemblance and figure of them, viz., of sinners.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=5&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And he said to me: That a house may be built for it in
               <u>
                the land of Sennaar
               </u>
               , and that it may be established, and set there upon its own basis.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [11]
               <u>
                "The land of Sennaar"
               </u>
               : Where Babel or Babylon was built, Gen. 11., where note, that Babylon in holy writ is often taken for the city of the devil: that is, for the whole congregation of the wicked: as Jerusalem is taken for the city and people of God.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               The vision of the four chariots. Crowns are ordered for Jesus the high priest, as a type of Christ.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               And I turned, and lifted up my eyes, and saw: and behold
               <u>
                four chariots
               </u>
               came out from the midst of two mountains: and the mountains were mountains of brass.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               In the first chariot were red horses, and in the second chariot black horses.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               And in the third chariot white horses, and in the fourth chariot grisled horses, and strong ones.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And I answered, and said to the angel that spoke in me: What are these, my lord?
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               And the angel answered, and said to me: These are the four winds of the heaven, which go forth to stand before the Lord of all the earth.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [1]
               <u>
                "Four chariots"
               </u>
               : The four great empires of the Chaldeans, Persians, Grecians, and Romans. Or perhaps by the fourth chariot are represented the kings of Egypt and of Asia, the descendants of Ptolemeus and Seleucus.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               That in which were the black horses went forth into
               <u>
                the land of the north
               </u>
               , and the white went forth after them: and the grisled went forth to
               <u>
                the land of the south
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               And they that were most strong, went out, and sought to go, and to run to and fro through all the earth. And he said: Go, walk throughout the earth: and they walked throughout the earth.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               And he called me, and spoke to me, saying: Behold they that go forth into the land of the north, have quieted my spirit in the land of the north.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               And the word of the Lord came to me, saying:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Take of them of the captivity, of Holdai, and of Tobias, and of Idaias; thou shalt come in that day, and shalt go into the house of Josias, the son of Sophonias, who came out of Babylon.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [6]
               <u>
                "The land of the north"
               </u>
               : So Babylon is called; because it lay to the north in respect of Jerusalem. The black horses, that is, the Medes and Persians: and after them Alexander and his Greeks, signified by the white horses, went thither because they conquered Babylon, executed upon it the judgments of God, which is signified, ver. 8, by the expression of quieting his spirit.-- Ibid.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [6]
               <u>
                "The land of the south"
               </u>
               : Egypt, which lay to the south of Jerusalem, and was occupied first by Ptolemeus, and then by the Romans.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               And thou shalt take gold and silver: and shalt make crowns, and thou shalt set them on the head of Jesus the son of Josedec, the high priest.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               And thou shalt speak to him, saying: Thus saith the Lord of hosts, saying: BEHOLD A MAN, THE ORIENT IS HIS NAME: and under him shall he spring up, and shall build a temple to the Lord.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               Yea, he shall build a temple to the Lord: and he shall bear the glory, and shall sit, and rule upon his throne: and he shall be a priest upon his throne, and the counsel of peace shall be
               <u>
                between them both
               </u>
               .
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               And the crowns shall be to Helem, and Tobias, and Idaias, and to Hem, the son of Sophonias, a memorial in the temple of the Lord.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=43&amp;ch=6&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               And they that are far off, shall come and shall build in the temple of the Lord: and you shall know that the Lord of hosts sent me to you. But this shall come to pass, if hearing you will hear the voice of the Lord your God.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [13]
               <u>
                "Between them both"
               </u>
               : That is, he shall unite in himself the two offices or dignities of king and priest.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>

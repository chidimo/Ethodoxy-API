             <td class="textarea" valign="top">
              <p class="desc">
               His charge to Timothy. He tells him of his approaching death and desires him to come to him.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=1-#x">
                [1]
               </a>
               I charge thee, before God and Jesus Christ, who shall judge the living and the dead, by his coming, and his kingdom:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=2-#x">
                [2]
               </a>
               Preach the word: be instant in season, out of season: reprove, entreat, rebuke in all patience and doctrine.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=3-#x">
                [3]
               </a>
               For there shall be a time, when they will not endure sound doctrine; but, according to their own desires, they will heap to themselves teachers, having itching ears:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=4-#x">
                [4]
               </a>
               And will indeed turn away their hearing from the truth, but will be turned unto fables.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=5-#x">
                [5]
               </a>
               But be thou vigilant, labour in all things, do the work of
               <u>
                an evangelist
               </u>
               , fulfill thy ministry. Be sober.
              </p>
              <p class="note">
               [5]
               <u>
                "An evangelist"
               </u>
               : a diligent preacher of the gospel.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=6-#x">
                [6]
               </a>
               For I am even now ready to be sacrificed: and the time of my dissolution is at hand.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=7-#x">
                [7]
               </a>
               I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the faith.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=8-#x">
                [8]
               </a>
               As to the rest, there is laid up for me a crown of justice, which the Lord the just judge will render to me in that day: and not only to me, but to them also that love his coming. Make haste to come to me quickly.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=9-#x">
                [9]
               </a>
               For Demas hath left me, loving this world, and is gone to Thessalonica:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=10-#x">
                [10]
               </a>
               Crescens into Galatia, Titus into Dalmatia.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=11-#x">
                [11]
               </a>
               Only Luke is with me. Take Mark, and bring him with thee: for he is profitable to me for the ministry.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=12-#x">
                [12]
               </a>
               But Tychicus I have sent to Ephesus.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=13-#x">
                [13]
               </a>
               The cloak that I left at Troas, with Carpus, when thou comest, bring with thee, and the books, especially the parchments.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=14-#x">
                [14]
               </a>
               Alexander the coppersmith hath done me much evil: the Lord will reward him according to his works:
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=15-#x">
                [15]
               </a>
               Whom do thou also avoid, for he hath greatly withstood our words.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=16-#x">
                [16]
               </a>
               At my first answer no man stood with me, but all forsook me: may it not be laid to their charge.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=17-#x">
                [17]
               </a>
               But the Lord stood by me, and strengthened me, that by me the preaching may be accomplished, and that all the Gentiles may hear: and I was delivered out of the mouth of the lion.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=18-#x">
                [18]
               </a>
               The Lord hath delivered me from every evil work: and will preserve me unto his heavenly kingdom, to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=19-#x">
                [19]
               </a>
               Salute Prisca and Aquila, and the household of Onesiphorus.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=20-#x">
                [20]
               </a>
               Erastus remained at Corinth. And Trophimus I left sick at Miletus.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=21-#x">
                [21]
               </a>
               Make haste to come before winter. Eubulus and Pudens, and Linus and Claudia, and all the brethren, salute thee.
               <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=62&amp;ch=4&amp;l=22-#x">
                [22]
               </a>
               The Lord Jesus Christ be with thy spirit. Grace be with you. Amen.
              </p>

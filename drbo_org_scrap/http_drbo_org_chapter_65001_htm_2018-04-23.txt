           <td class="textarea" valign="top">
            <p class="desc">
             God spoke of old by the prophets, but now by his Son, who is incomparably greater than the angels.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=1-#x">
              [1]
             </a>
             God, who, at sundry times and in divers manners, spoke in times past to the fathers by the prophets, last of all,
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=2-#x">
              [2]
             </a>
             In these days hath spoken to us by his Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the world.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=3-#x">
              [3]
             </a>
             Who being the brightness of his glory, and
             <u>
              the figure
             </u>
             of his substance, and upholding all things by the word of his power,
             <u>
              making purgation
             </u>
             of sins, sitteth on the right hand of the majesty on high.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=4-#x">
              [4]
             </a>
             Being made so much better than the angels, as he hath inherited a more excellent name than they.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=5-#x">
              [5]
             </a>
             For to which of the angels hath he said at any time, Thou art my Son, today have I begotten thee? And again, I will be to him a Father, and he shall be to me a Son?
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [3]
             <u>
              "The figure"
             </u>
             : that is, the express image, and most perfect resemblance.
            </p>
            <p class="note">
             [3]
             <u>
              "Making purgation"
             </u>
             : That is, having purged away our sins by his passion.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=6-#x">
              [6]
             </a>
             And again, when he bringeth in the first begotten into the world, he saith: And let all the angels of God adore him.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=7-#x">
              [7]
             </a>
             And to the angels indeed he saith: He that maketh his angels spirits, and his ministers a flame of fire.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=8-#x">
              [8]
             </a>
             But to the Son: Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever: a sceptre of justice is the sceptre of thy kingdom.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=9-#x">
              [9]
             </a>
             Thou hast loved justice, and hated iniquity: therefore God, thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=10-#x">
              [10]
             </a>
             And: Thou in the beginning, O Lord, didst found the earth: and the works of thy hands are the heavens.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=11-#x">
              [11]
             </a>
             They shall perish, but thou shalt continue: and they shall all grow old as a garment.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=12-#x">
              [12]
             </a>
             And as a vesture shalt thou change them, and they shall be changed: but thou art the selfsame, and thy years shall not fail.
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=13-#x">
              [13]
             </a>
             But to which of the angels said he at any time: Sit on my right hand, until I make thy enemies thy footstool?
             <a href="/x/d?b=drb&amp;bk=65&amp;ch=1&amp;l=14-#x">
              [14]
             </a>
             Are they not all ministering spirits, sent to minister for them, who shall receive the inheritance of salvation?
            </p>
